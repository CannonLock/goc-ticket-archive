    May 26, 2010 05:10 PM UTC by Doug Olson 
    Here is a reply from ESnet on May 14 that did not get back to the OSG ticket.
The basic answer being that the affected CRL is not being served from a
pool of servers at ESnet but only a single source so perhaps the difficulty
is with proxy servers at FNAL.

from	Mike Helm <helm@....>
reply-to	helm@....
to	ESnet OPS <operator@....>
cc	Doug Olson <dlolson@....>,
Dhiva <dhiva@....>,
"Roberto R. Morelli" <morelli@....>,
ESnet Noc <trouble@....>,
pki-team@....
date	Fri, May 14, 2010 at 1:04 PM
subject	Re: [trouble] TTS: 22087 - Assigned to Dhiva - for FNAL - ESNET CRL Issue - Open Science Grid
hide details May 14 (12 days ago)
I talked to Roberto in the hall, here at LBL, & he says that
the symptoms reported to us are characteristic of a broken F5
service (which can act as a proxy), one of the pieces not
functioning, or possibly a broken web proxy somewhere (like
mentioned earlier), where the daemon is still up but the
update service failed.

ESnet does not have public redundancy for the www.es.net/es.net web
servers as yet.

We don't have enough information on the problem to diagnose
exactly what the FNAL engineer saw, but the  owners of
" pool of (?independent?) web servers" should be
identified.

Hope this is useful.

Thanks, ==mwh

    May 13, 2010 04:09 PM UTC by LBNL-DSD-support 
    Hi ESnet,
Fermilab has had trouble downloading the CRL for the ESnet Root CA from
http://es.net/CA/d1b603c3/CRL/d1b603c3.r0.
Below  is the report form Keith Chadwick.
Doug

According to the FermiGrid monitoring, over the past couple of days,
there have been problems downloading the CRL (d1b603c3.r0) for the
ESnet CA (d1b603c3.0).

This appears to have started on or about 11-May-2010 11:15 Central and
continued through 13-May-2010 01:21 Central.

The information for the currently released CRL is:

openssl crl -in /etc/grid-security/certificates/d1b603c3.r0 -text | grep
-i update
Last Update: Apr  8 17:07:21 2010 GMT
Next Update: Apr  8 17:07:21 2011 GMT

Investigation at FermiGrid indicates that the ESnet CRL contact URL
(http://es.net/CA/d1b603c3/CRL/d1b603c3.r0) is apparently being
served by a pool of (?independent?) web servers and at least one of the
web servers appears to have been serving a copy of an stale/obsolete
CRL that had a Last Update of Nov 2009 and a Next Update of Nov 2010.

-Keith.
