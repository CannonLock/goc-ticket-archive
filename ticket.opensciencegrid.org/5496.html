    Sep 4, 2008 03:15 PM UTC by Tim Silvers 
    Arvind,

I'm CC'ing Rob. I don't think you understand my concern. If we're
going to have a special case where we deviate from our normal,
documented procedures, *we* (I and Elizabeth) need to know. We will
need to communicate perhaps with the GRNOC staff, who also process
tickets. Having a special process for BDII testing isn't a problem as
long as we all know it is happening and we know how to deal with it.
And until we can define new SOPs, I suggest we stick to what we have
currently. A lot of changes have been/are being made for the
betterment of OIM and OSG, but that isn't a reason to disregard
procedures. I'm happy to develop new procedures, but I will need input
from you, Rob or others on what those procedures should be. Hopefully
we can hash this out in our late September meetings.

Tim

    Sep 4, 2008 02:42 PM UTC by Tim Silvers 
    Arvind,

If you're going to activate a Resource, sending the update to the GOC
is okay (updating the FP ticket is preferable), but your note should
not be ignored. Anytime we activate a Resource, ITB or otherwise, it
needs to be included in the related FP ticket. Activating a Resource
in OIM is a part of our SOP for processing registrations and as such
it needs to be recorded in the FP ticket so that I, or whomever is
processing the request, will know that all steps have been completed
for activating the registration. I will now proceed with adding this
Resource to monitoring.

Thanks,

Tim
