    Apr 9, 2007 04:41 PM UTC by VDT 
    Hi Ashutosh,
Thanks for bringing this to our attention.  I've figured out where the
problem comes from, and I have a few questions for you.

1) Based on your path, I think you are running Debian 3.1 on a 64 bit
machine.  Is this correct?
2) Did you use the stock installation of perl from Debian, or did you
build your own?

The reason I ask is that the perl architecture string
(x86_64-linux-gnu-thread-multi) does not match what we have for a
standard Debian 3.1, 32-bit install.  We currently do not support (and
do not have a test machine) for Debian 3.1 on 64-bit, which might be why
we are seeing this issue for the first time.

Your solution of creating symlinks is the right way to deal with this.
Have you had any further problems since creating the symlinks?

We will be adding a check in a future version of the VDT to verify that
the PERL5LIB path seems correct with the given architecture.

Let me know if you have any further questions.
Scot
