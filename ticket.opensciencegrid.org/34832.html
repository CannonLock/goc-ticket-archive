    Nov 17, 2017 11:08 PM UTC by chuckles@.... 
    Received communication from Stefan at belwue.de, the ISP that provides
services to uni-freiburg.de. They apparently merge Internet and research
networking route tables together and follow the standard BGP path decision
tree to route their traffic. In this case the route path over their ISP
links is better than the one to us via the research networks. So they are
stuck. Unlike here, we provide our clients with separate peering for the
research networks so each client can make their own routing decisions on how
best to deliver traffic and we can automatically prefer the research network
path. So to correct this we need to investigate route prepending but need to
selectively apply this. We will look into making the necessary change and
getting it approved through our change advisory board as soon as possible.

Saul Youssef reached out to us for assistance with this issue. As I
understand things, the site 132.230.203.210 is unable to reach atlas.bu.edu via
ICMP. From what we can tell there is an asymmetric route going on between
the two hosts. Traceroutes from the host atlas.bu.edu to the host
132.230.203.210 show the traffic leaving the atlas.bu.edu cluster via its
facility and being passed to our research network peer the Northern
Crossroads (NoX – 18.122.64.1 in traceroute below) which carries it to
Internet2 and then over to GEANT to be delivered to 132.230.203.210. The
traceroute we received from this ticket that I believe is from
132.230.203.210 to atlas.bu.edushows that it is responding over the
commodity Internet instead of following the research networks back to
atlas.bu.edu (Cogent is one of our commodity Internet ISP’s). For some
reason, the host 132.230.203.210 is preferring a path back to us using the
commodity Internet. Can you check the local router to see if it has a route
to atlas.bu.edu (192.5.207.230) that it is also learning via its research
network peer? If not, then we have to try and track down where this is
being lost in the route path. If it is learning the route via the research
network peer, then is there any way to have that router at that site
configured to prefer the path it is learning over the research network? For
example, set a “local pref” for routes via that research peer)?

    
The reason ICMP is not getting through is because the asymmetric return
path that comes to us via the commodity Internet is going through our
campus edge firewall which does not allow inbound sourced ICMP traffic. The
firewall allows traffic sourced from the campus to initiate ICMP to outside
sites but not the other way around. If we can correct the asymmetry and
move this traffic onto the research network path then we can probably solve
two issues, the ICMP issue because we do not block this via the research
network path, and potentially bandwidth and cost issues since we pay far
less for research bandwidth then commodity internet bandwidth.

Does this make sense or do you think we are misinterpreting what we are
seeing?
