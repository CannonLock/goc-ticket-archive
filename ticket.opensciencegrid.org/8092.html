    Feb 17, 2010 09:32 PM UTC by jrlee@.... 
    Hello Ruth,

Please let me know if the following meets your needs:

The Daya Bay organization will be adding additional resources to several
sites. First, NERSC's PDSF (Parallel Distributed Systems Facility) which
is a shared computing cluster resource that is used by several existing
OSG partners. Secondly at BNL's RACF (RHIC and ATLAS Computing Facility)
cluster which is also utilized by several grid projects. Daya Bay will
also be inter-operating with many grid enabled universities such as;
Wisconsin, Urbana-Champaign, Princeton, etc. to name a few. These
universities will be here in the US and in China. Daya Bay will leverage
off of the existing  amount of grid enabled services at these various
institutions to allow for a efficient and fairly seamless work-flow to
occur. Daya Bay expects the use of a Virtual Organization to provide
a path forward for this framework. At this time, we are expecting that
certificate-authenticated data transfers in both directions will occur,
but that no certificate-authenticated job-scheduling between US and CN
resources is foreseen.

The current state of the Daya Bay operation is in beta. Currently
Daya Bay has monte carlo simulations running of the code on various
existing clusters. We are also using some grid services to provide
data movement (gridftp) in a automated manner. Daya Bay will evolve
into to a system where large-scale units of work would be distributed
with a grid-enabled system to institutions' clusters where the local
batch system would then take responsibility for scheduling individual
jobs.

    Feb 8, 2010 07:07 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    Thank you for registering the DayaBay Virtual Organization. By completing the VO Registration form you have started the approval process to have your VO added to the OSG.  This process should take approximately 7 business days to complete.

Your VO will be presented for approval to the OSG Council on the first Thursday after it is submitted and then sent to the next Operations Workgroup for discussion on the next Monday. If you would like to attend the Operations Workgroup meeting to represent your VO and inform the community of your participation and goal in joining the OSG you can find the details at http://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Operations/ProductionMeetingMinutes.

There are several mailing lists associated with the OSG, as a new VO you may want to subscribe to the following mailing lists:

osg@....  -- For general OSG announcements
osg-general@....  -- For general technical discussion and open community support
osg-operations@....  -- Operations activities
osg-ops-status@....  -- OSG Status Reports
vo-admins@....  -- OSG VO and GUMS Administration

Details for subscribing to these lists and a list of other OSG mailing lists you may be interested in subscribing to can be found at http://www.opensciencegrid.org/Consortium_Mailing_Lists.

Please contact the Grid Operations Center (GOC) at goc@.... if you have any questions.

Thank you for your interest in the OSG, and we look forward to your participation in the OSG Consortium!
