    Jul 23, 2008 02:00 PM UTC by TIGRE 
    I believe that the ultimate solution in this case is to have the GOC
annotate their forwarding of the VDT update announcements to flag
updates that apply to the standard released packages.  For example,
the GOC version of the update notice might say.

This lettered version includes updates to the following packages that
are part of standard OSG packages:

CE:

xxx xxx xxx

SE:

xxx xxx xxx

Just forwarding the VDT updates is not as good as telling us in a bit
more detail what we should typically be looking to update, in addition
to the general announcement.

The reason many of us got tripped up, I believe, by "Gratia-Reporting"
is that it sounds like something we might want as part of a CE, for
example.  I know (and have known for some time) about how to look for
what is installed and how to compare this (manually, tediously one at
a time) to what is in a particular set of updates, but this one
sounded very much like an omission.  (As it turns out, it was not.)

Hope this helps,
Alan

The VDT announcement is a general announcement for everything in the
VDT, and it's not specific to a subset. If you don't have the
Gratia-Reporting package, then don't update it.

Most CE installations in OSG don't have Gratia-Reporting. It's the
"Gratia Collector", which lets you collect statistics locally. Not many
people are using it yet, but they could use it, and it might even be
installed on their CE.

I'm sorry the directions weren't more clear!
