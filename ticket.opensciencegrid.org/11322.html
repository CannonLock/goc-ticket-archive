    Dec 27, 2011 03:25 AM UTC 
    Actually,FermiGrid has implemented a SYSTEM_PERIODIC_REMOVE expression in condor
to kill high-memory job. limits.conf doesn't work for this purpose because condor doesn't
use PAM and therefore doesn't look at limits.conf.  When we transition to
condor 7.6 or higher we will transition to kill on resident set size.
We haven't seen any more problematic jobs from gluex but even if they do come now
we are ready for them.  This ticket can be closed.

Steve Timm

    Richard, we have added a ImageSize>10000000 constraint to our condor on our
general purpose clusters such that any jobs in future which go over that
memory usage (10GB) will automatically be killed.  This has been known
to produce some false positives in the past as condor 7.4 is not very good
at memory counting and tends to overcount.  The gluex jobs of today however
were legit memory hogs.

    [by Steve Timm]

We are seeing 60-70 jobs from the gluex VO on the FNAL_FERMIGRID site
that are using huge memory, between 17GB to 32GB of RAM per job.
We have not yet been able to track them to a specific gluex user or
to catch one in the act before it crashes one of our nodes but
we are still investigating.

We have added a limit of 10GB per job to all of our clusters which means
all these jobs will be killed.  If new ones like this don't stop coming
shortly we will start banning other gluex users and killing jobs.

Steve Timm
