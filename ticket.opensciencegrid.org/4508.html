    Hey John,

It's a bit difficult to discuss via tickets, but here's my best answers:

1) It's the list of VOs which are registered with the OSG via the
registration DB.  Basically, I want to get rid of all the "crap VOs"
which are really just configuration mistakes from the sites.

I assume that vo_txt.php is the authoritative source, as it comes from
the registration DB.  However, for interoperability concerns, I would
be very careful about changing the capitalization without first
testing to see if it breaks reporting to the LCG.  In fact, I would be
happier if we just got the "list" correct for now in Gratia, and worry
about capitalization later.

I'm not too worry about breaking MonALISA, as this has been long-
deprecated.

2) I would define "significant CPU usage" as more than 10 hours a year
which are not configuration errors and officially sanctioned VOs as
those which registered in the registration DB.  I want to deliberately
keep "significant CPU usage" ridiculously low so we don't
inadvertently insult anyone.

Gratia has recorded 106 VOs in the production instance.  I suspect we
can filter it down to about 50 without even trying, of which maybe 20
or so are registered.  We don't remove the other 30; rather, we try
and get them registered.

Right now, I would like to get rid of VOs which are obviously
removable (see my post to the gratia-ops list; I'll recreate the list
below).

This is the reason I'm interested in filtering out the VO list:


    http://t2.unl.edu:8086/vo_overview

Brian
