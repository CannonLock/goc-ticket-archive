    Aug 31, 2010 10:45 PM UTC by echism 
    Marco,

A brief history, and this is a touch tedious, but I hope you will
appreciate the pay-off here:

At one time, our SOP was as follows:

1) register (correctly and completely) with the GOC via registration db
2) run one clean scan of site_verify.pl
3) attend the operations call
4) get entered into VORS monitoring (based on site_verify, the
requirement was to both promote the health of the OSG and to prevent
our attention from being repeatedly drawn to an unprepared resource)

Reg DB was deprecated and surpassed by OIM. Additional requirements at
this point:

1) Have a personal IGTF certificate
2) Register in OIM (Reg DB requirement dropped)

VORS was completely deprecated, after a long grace period requested by
the community, and MyOSG became the monitoring system. There were no
SOP changes for a period, but eventually:

1) site_verify.pl requirement was deemed outdated for our purposes.
2) the expanded amount of information in OIM (as compared to Reg DB)
seemed to be sufficient in most cases to confirm a relationship with
OSG.
3) We activated the resources as soon as the information was complete
and accurate to our standards, and used the operations call to inform
the community.

Now, as far as Duke is concerned, I seem to recall that they
registered slightly before the 2009 all hands meeting. This was quite
some time ago, and I believe that they were in the second iteration of

    our SOP that I described.

As far as the documentation, this document is (and has been) on my
list, so it's not a major problem to prioritize it. I was still
gathering a complete list of the operations documents that I needed to
work on, but I can certainly expedite this one and *then* go back to
doing that. Could we perhaps have a short phone discussion on the
subject tomorrow?

Thank you,
Elizabeth

    Aug 31, 2010 08:41 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    The current OIM registration process requires resource owners to attend the GOC operation meeting to activate a resource newly added in OIM. The resource remains inactive until the owner attends the meeting.

At the last Tier 3 liaison meeting USATLAS asked to simplify this process for Tier 3 resources, specially for the one that will provide only storage elements and not a fully functional site.

The proposal is that a direct communication to GOC from the Tier 3 coordinators (i.e. Rik and Doug for USATLAS) could provide the required verification.
