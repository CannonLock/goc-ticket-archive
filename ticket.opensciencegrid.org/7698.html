    Hi Tanya-

On Thu, Oct 29, 2009 at 07:16:31AM -0500, Tanya Levshina wrote:
I have forwarded your emails to Vladimir. Hopefully he will be
able to look at your problem first thing in the morning.

Fortunately, there's no need. With Brian's help, we were able to
discover the problem: there was a rogue pnfsDomain in our cluster.
Back on 2009.07.02, we briefly moved our PNFS service to our
hotspare machine when the hardware on the main machine failed. When
we moved the service to a new machine later that week, we neglected
to unlink node_config or remove the dcache init script from the boot
order (though we did turn the service off).

We recovered successfully from Tuesday night's hardware failure and
ran without problems until mid-afternoon on Wednesday. Our problems
began when we rebooted the hotspare machine so it could switch to a
new kernel. This reboot started up the PNFS server on that machine
as well, confusing the central services to no end. This morning,
Brian noticed that the cell services page showed a pnfsDomain even
though all of the dCache services on our PNFS server were turned
off. This lead us to look at the old server and discover the
problem.

This seems like a fairly rare and unlikely event, but improvements
to the logging messages in this case would certainly help other
sites should they face this problem. In no case did any of the logs
indicate that there were multiple pnfsDomains. While it might not be
easy for the services to realize that there are multiple active
domains, including the IP address or hostname in log messages
concerning the services would have made this clear from the start.
Additionally, it would have helped if dCache had simply chosen one
of the services and isolated the other. Even if it chose the wrong
service, the nature of the problem would have become clear to us
more quickly.

    
Thanks for your help, everybody!

Our PnfsManager is failing to start up.  We have not been able to figure out what the problem is.

We restarted PnfsManager earlier today to fix a problem causing the cleaner to not function, because there was an lock file left over from some
previous time.  This restart was successful and resulted in the cleaner starting to do a lot of work.  Then later in the day we found that we could not
restart pools.  They complained about not being able to do pnfs queries.  So we restarted PnfsManager, but from that point on, PnfsManager does not
appear to be functioning properly.  We moved the trash/2 directory out of the way and created an empty directory, but that did not help.

We are using dcache 1.9.0-8.

PnfsManager runs on cmsdcache01.hep.wisc.edu.
