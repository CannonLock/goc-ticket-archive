    Hi Richard,

Let me shine some light on your confusion. At many sites, the admins have several different queues set up on their gatekeepers, and possibly even several gatekeepers, each of which might have its own properties (such as MaxWalltime, supported VOs, operating system, etc.). In the glidein factories, we have several logical "entries", each of which has a unique name, and points to a specific queue on a specific gatekeeper. (To make this concrete, I'll refer you to our factory monitoring page: http://gfactory-1.t2.ucsd.edu/osg_gfactory/factoryStatusNow.html. There you can see all our currently active entries). In many cases, we have multiple entries that point to the same CE, just different queues, or with different properties.

If I quickly filter the factory monitoring page, I see that Gluex is currently submitting glideins to 3 different entries in Brazil. I've included the MaxWalltime associated with each entry as well:
CMS_T2_BR_SPRACE (31.9 hrs)
HCC_BR_UNESP (23.5 hrs)
OSG_BR_UNESP_medium (23.5 hrs)

The latter two entries point to the same gatekeeper, but different queues. I checked the glidein logs, and the nodes behind all 3 entries have the same naming format: node###.local. I'm not sure whether these nodes are all in the same pool, or whether the name similarity is just a coincidence. But either way, it would seem the difference in walltime is due to your glideins going to different factory entries, and thus different gatekeepers (SPRACE vs UNESP).

Anyway, with that explained, I'm going to look and see why your glideins are having trouble at UNESP, and I'll get back to you shortly!

Cheers,
Dan Klein
UCSD Glidein Factory Operations
