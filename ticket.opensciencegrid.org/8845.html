    Jul 7, 2010 09:23 PM UTC by Burt Holzman 
    Hi (cc to vdt-support and condor-admin):

There is an interaction between the GID tracking option of the Condor
batch system on the Tier 1 and the glexec tracking GIDs.  Essentially,
the gidd_alloc process reparents itself to init, and sets its GID to one
of the glexec tracking GIDs (discarding the Condor tracking GID).  If
the job exits normally, the glexec monitor terminates the gidd_alloc
process; otherwise the job is terminated via condor_rm or condor_hold,
Condor doesn't realize that gidd_alloc is part of the process tree and
it lingers forever.

This could be obviated if gidd_alloc added the glexec tracking GID as
another supplemental GID (although that might be a privilege leak if the
VO and user jobs rely on GID-based file permissions); or to maintain
the PPID to the condor_exec process.
