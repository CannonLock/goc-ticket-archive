Alain, Terrance & Others,

In order to better fulfill our fiduciary duty in the management of
DOE owned computers, Fermilab computing management made the decision
to base one facet of our grid security plan on specific trust
relationships between Fermilab and Virtual Organizations rather than
between Fermilab and specific individuals.

This policy has been announced and discussed in several forums (including
the recent OSG Consortium Meeting from August 21-24, 2006 in Seattle).

The Fermilab SAZ service is the mechanism by which the users job is
vetted against the trust relationships between Fermilab and Virtual
Organizations.

There were several announcements on various OSG lists inviting people
to try the FNAL_FERMIGRID_TEST compute element which was validating jobs
against the Fermilab SAZ service for more than a month prior to the
going "live" date of FNAL_FERMIGRID.

Approximately 16 hours after we went live with FNAL_FERMIGRID validating
jobs against the Fermilab SAZ service, I opened a ticket with the OSG GOC
to let the Nanohub VO support center know that their jobs were failing
the Fermilab SAZ service validation.  Here is the ticket header which
was sent to me:

Project: Open Science Grid
Issue: 2577

    Assignees: OSG Support Centers, nanoHUB, Tim Silvers
Customer Impact: 4-Normal
Status: Service Desk
Ticket-Type: Problem/Request
Title: Nanohub job failures at FNAL_FERMIGRID
Submitted on: 10/03/2006 18:49:33
Submitted by: Tim Silvers
Originating Ticket Number:
Destination Ticket Number:

The reason why we are requiring voms-proxy-init is that grid-proxy-init
does not provide enough information to the Fermilab gatekeepers to
uniquely identify the user requesting the service as being a valid
member of a Virtual Organization with which we have a trust relationship.
The possibilities include:

1.  The user is not a member of any VO.
2.  The user is a member of only one VO.
3.  The user is a member of more than one VO.

The requirement for voms-proxy-init solves these issues (at least at Fermilab).
The user select the VO that they wish their job to execute under, and that
information is securely passed to all Globus gatekeepers (not just those
at Fermilab).

At Fermilab, if the user does not perform a voms-proxy-init with a VO's VOMS
server, then we treat the user as coming from the "NULL" VO, and the "NULL"
VO is specifically disabled by the Fermilab SAZ service, consequently any
jobs submitted by the user to FNAL_FERMIGRID will be rejected.

It is important to note that at the moment, only the FNAL_FERMIGRID and
FNAL_FERMIGRID_TEST gatekeepers are configured to use the Fermilab SAZ service.
Other gatekeepers at Fermilab are not (yet) configured to use the Fermilab
SAZ service - but you can expect that they will be configured to use the SAZ
service in the near future.

-Keith.

    Oct 3, 2006 06:49 PM UTC by Tim Silvers 
    Dear Nanohub VO Support Center,

Following the deployment of the Site AuthoriZation (SAZ) service
at Fermilab, I have been watching closely the Globus jobs which
the Fermilab SAZ has failed to authorize.

Of the (to date) 230 jobs which have failed to authorized by SAZ,
208 belong to the Nanohub VO.

Looking at the SAZ service log, it appears that these faild Nanohub
VO jobs are being submitted using grid proxy credentials generated
by grid-proxy-init.

In order for the Nanohub VO jobs to be successfully run at Fermilab,
you will need to revise your job submission scripts to utilize
grid proxy credentials generated by voms-proxy-init.

Please feel free to contact me if you have any questions regarding
this change.

Thanks.
