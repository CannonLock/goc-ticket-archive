    Hi Jon,

Our policy is to try to avoid recomputations whenever possible. Only in
cases when the problem is easy to correct and site(s) are significantly
affected, we've done the effort. If the request comes in long after the
reports have been distributed, we usually don't accept them.

I've spent some time reviewing the FNAL numbers and for this particular
case, the computed availability is 97%, while if not penalized it would
have been 97.19%, which rounded would be the same, 97%.
On the other hand, the computed reliability is 98%, while if not
penalized it would have been 98.52%, which rounded would be 99%.

As the availability and reliability target is >= 97%, the difference is
just 1% in reliability, and the reports have been already distributed, I
don't see a valid reason for recomputing your numbers (taking also into
account the time that several people must spend in the process.)
I hope you understand.

Best regards,
David
