> single DN even if the DN never accesses the computing resources, with

> run out quickly even if there are thousands of accounts in the
> pool. And since most of the DNs are actually inactive, assigning them
> accounts wastes a lot of resources on both gatekeepers and worknodes
> and can slow down the performance of the whole system. I know we can
> use group account mapping instead, but group account mapping lacks
> some good features of one-to-one account pool mapping, which we
> need. I just wonder if there is a better way other than switching to
> group account mapping to handle this scalability problem.
>

    Jul 16, 2008 07:57 PM UTC by echism 
    Hi,

We are using account pool mapping in our GUMS. We noticed a scalability problem with
it: since GUMS assigns account to every single DN even if the DN never accesses the
computing resources, with the rapid increase of number of DNs in some VOs the account
pool can run out quickly even if there are thousands of accounts in the pool. And
since most of the DNs are actually inactive, assigning them accounts wastes a lot of
resources on both gatekeepers and worknodes and can slow down the performance of the
whole system. I know we can use group account mapping instead, but group account
mapping lacks some good features of one-to-one account pool mapping, which we need. I
just wonder if there is a better way other than switching to group account mapping to
handle this scalability problem.

Thanks,

Yu
