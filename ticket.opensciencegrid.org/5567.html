    The VDT is releasing an important update that affects both existing and new
installations of VDT 1.8.1 (OSG 0.8) and VDT 1.10.1 (OSG 1.0). Please read the
following carefully. If anything isn't clear, please feel free to contact us.

As previously announced, new VDT installations of VDT 1.10.1 and later will no longer
install CA certificates by default. This means that if you don't perform a couple of
simple steps, *** new installations will not work ***. As you probably know, CA
certificates are essential for nearly all software components in the VDT, for both
clients and servers.

If you have an existing installation, it will continue to work during our transition
period (approximately two weeks) even if you do not take the software update.
However, after the transition period, you will have to install the software update or
you will not receive CA certificate updates.

Why are we doing this? The VDT installation (outside of the OSG software stack) is
intended to be a grid-agnostic software stack, but the set of Certificate Authorities
you trust is a combination of the grid you a member of and what CAs you personally
trust. By making these changes, we do two things:

1) We require users to think--even if briefly--about what set of CAs they trust.
2) The VDT team will no longer be in the business of recommending CAs to Open Science
Grid sites. Instead, that duty belongs to the OSG Security Team, who develops the CA
distribution and hosts it on the GOC's web site.

For the foreseeable future, the VDT will provide a convenience distribution of CA
certificates. For now, that will continue to be the IGTF accredited CAs, plus the
Fermi KCA, plus the TeraGrid CAs. In the near future (the date will be announced by
the OSG Security Team), the VDT convenience CA distribution will become just the IGTF
accredited CAs. However, you (the user) will be required to select this distribution
if it is what you want instead of another distribution. The VDT Team will provide
this as a convenience, but not as a recommendation.

The software update released in VDT 1.10.1 and VDT 1.8.1 gives you the ability to
select between two CA Distribution services (VDT or OSG GOC). Without the software

    update, you cannot specify which CA distribution service you want to receive from.
Therefore, after our transition period you will not receive CA certificate updates.

Again to make the transition smooth, the VDT convenience distribution of CA
certificates will continue to provide the current set of CAs (IGTF, TeraGrid, and
Fermilab KCA) for the foreseeable future. During this transition, you will need to
select which source you will get CA certificate from (VDT or OSG GOC) so that when
the VDT does drop the non-IGTF accredited CAs, it will not cause any unexpected
disruptions in service.

In order to make encourage this transition, the VDT will relocate its CA distribution
to a new web address, and this will force VDT 1.8.1 users to update their CA
certificate updater. Note that the old address will not disappear during our
transition period.

===== VDT 1.10.1 (OSG 1.0) =====
If you use vdt-update-certs, you MUST update in order to continue getting CA
certificate updates.

Please see the update instructions to get VDT 1.10.1i at
<http://vdt.cs.wisc.edu/releases/1.10.1/release-i.html >. These instructions tell you
how to reconfigure the VDT to point at a CA certificate distribution.

If you've installed VDT 1.10.1 by installing the OSG 1.0 CE installation (or any
other installation from the OSG cache), you will follow these same instructions.

Exception: if you use the VDT's RPM (or yum repository) for installing the CA
certificates, please continue using it for now. The OSG GOC will soon host a yum
repository and it will be announced when it is ready.

===== VDT 1.8.1 (OSG 0.8) =====
If you use vdt-update-certs, you MUST update in order to continue getting CA
certificate updates. If you update your CA certificates with Pacman, that will
continue to work for the foreseeable future with no change on your part.

Please see the update instructions to get VDT 1.8.1q at
<http://vdt.cs.wisc.edu/releases/1.8.1/release-q.html >. These instructions tell you
how to reconfigure the VDT to point at a CA certificate distribution.

If you've installed VDT 1.8.1 by installing any OSG 0.8 package, you will need to
follow these same directions: the OSG installation process hasn't been changed.

===== VDT 1.6.1 (OSG 0.6) =====
For VDT 1.6.1, there was only the Pacman update method. This will be supported for
the foreseeable future with no change on your part.

===== Making your own CA certificate distribution =====
If you want to make your own CA certificate distribution for your grid, to support a
specific set of CAs, you can do that. At this time, we have not documented the
procedure, but we expect to do so in the near future.
