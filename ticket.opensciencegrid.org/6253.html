New Work Log Entry :
OSG VO

Please review this request from Ashot Melikyan:

I would appreciate if you could grant me admission to the "OSG" VO.

In the immediate future I will be using it for small-scale
calculations that do not require significant
resources. Please let me know if you require the description of my research
projects.
I have never used Open Science Grid. My
experience with grid computing includes HPC cluster at the University
of Florida from 2004-2006, and more recently (2006-2008) Monolith and
Jazz clusters at Argonne National Lab. All three clusters use PBS
queueing and job management system.

None of the projects I am involved in will be using true
parallelization. At the moment, all projects I am involved in require
running many similar jobs with different parameters.

One of the projects (in collaboration with Konstantin Matveev from
Argonne National Laboratory) is a Quantum Monte Carlo study of
quasi-one-dimensional quantum wires.
In quasi-1D systems QMC are significantly simpler than in higher
dimensions since the fermion problem is non-existent,
as the zeros of the fermionic wave functions are known in advance, and
Diffusion Monte-Carlo is essentially exact.
We are interested in relatively small-size systems, because the exact
size-scaling analysis based on conformal theory
allows one to extract accurately all physical quantities of interest,
e.g. the velocities of collective charge and spin excitations. The
code we developed is currently at the testing stage.

    
The second project I have been actively pursuing in collaboration with
Oskar Vafek from Florida State University is the study of vortices in
high-temperature superconductors. Lately, there has been a renewed
interest in the problem due to the quantum oscillations observed by
the group of L. Taillefer and others in the vortex-liquid state of
YBCO and BSCCO. In this project, the numerically intensive portion of
our computations involves diagonalization of large sparse matrices for
a large number of parameters.

I am not quite sure whether you require a more formal proposal at this
stage that includes our previous related publications
and other information. If you do, I would appreciate if you could let
me know the preferred format.

Thanking you in advance,

Ashot
