    Oct 13, 2014 04:23 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    It was suggested that I may not have had enough glexec groups; however, the nodes that were working were my 24 core ones and not the other way around. So the next thing I checked were the permissions and everything was the same. so I reviewed the error messages from /var/log/messages and compared it to the ones shown in section 4.2 of https://twiki.opensciencegrid.org/bin/view/Documentation/Release3/TroubleshootingGlexecLcmaps . This time I noticed that rather than having an error initializing glexectracking I had one for lcmaps_localaccount.mod . I was going to check its permissions but I could not find it via locate on the problem nodes but I could on the working ones. I the tried "rpm -qa | grep lcmap" on a working and a problem node and notice that the problem node was missing RPMs. I next tried yum info  and found version 1.6.1, from epel, on the problem node and 1.5.7, from osg, on the working node. So I used yum to remove lcmaps and it removed most of the osg-wn-client-glexec RPMs. I next reinstalled the osg-wn-client-glexec and copied the /etc/lcmaps.db from the working nodes and now glexec seems to be working. Thanks for the help.I finally found the solution and now it seems to be working. The problem was I mistakenly installed lcmaps from epel before I installed osg-wn-client-glexec and it blocked some other lcmaps modules on my older, 2-core, node, which I cloned to all the older nodes, but did it correctly on the newer 24-core nodes. Thus it was only failing on my older nodes. I fixed this Friday afternoon but I wanted to wait a few day to make sure it was working.After determining that I had the wrong version of lcmaps installed I used yum to remove lcmaps and it removed most of the osg-wn-client-glexec RPMs. I next reinstalled the osg-wn-client-glexec and copied the /etc/lcmaps.db from the working nodes and now glexec seems to be working.
