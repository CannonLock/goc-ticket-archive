it hands off to another part of GIP.  I've attached a patch

Could you do the following:
1. Switch to htcondor-ce in your osg configs (10-gateway.ini)

3. Apply the attached patch with -p1 in the /usr/lib/python2.6/site-packages directory
4. Run gip_info
5. Attach /tmp/gip_osg_cp1.cfg and /tmp/gip_osg_cp2.cfg to this ticket
6. Restore the original gip_osg.py
7. Switch back to GRAM in your osg configs

    I just updated our CE to 3.2.21 in hopes to begin sending HTCondorCE information to the various BDII systems so our CMS user applications would begin submitted via that grid resource manager instead of GRAM.  After the update I noticed that our site is now failing the GIP Validation Status in MyOSG, http://tinyurl.com/pvur75p.

The error: (ForeignKey Check) GLUE Entity GlueCEUniqueID=ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:2119/jobmanager-slurm-background-4g does not exist for GlueClusterUniqueID=ce01.brazos.tamu.edu,Mds-Vo-name=TAMU_BRAZOS,Mds-Vo-name=local,o=grid.

I looked at the output from gip_info and noticed something strange, the GlueClusterUniqueID [1] LDIF entry has these 3 lines:

GlueForeignKey: GlueCEUniqueID=ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:2119/jobmanager-slurm-hepx
GlueForeignKey: GlueCEUniqueID=ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:2119/jobmanager-slurm-background
GlueForeignKey: GlueCEUniqueID=ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:2119/jobmanager-slurm-background-4g

Every other reference to GlueCEUniqueID in the LDIF output is using the htcondorce paths like the following:

GlueCEUniqueID: ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:9619/htcondorce-slurm-hepx
GlueCEUniqueID: ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:9619/htcondorce-slurm-background
GlueCEUniqueID: ce01.brazos.tamu.edu:9619/htcondorce-slurm-background-4g

I'm unsure if this is a bug or a configuration issue on my end.  The person from our local CMS group that I work with to ensure their software works is reporting that the lcg-info commands he uses to validate our site are returning incomplete data and when he runs CRAB the application can not find our resource. Usually this is just propagation delay, at least it has been in the past, but when I see GIP validation errors I'd assume that's the culprit.
