    Oct 21, 2008 03:41 PM UTC by Scott Kronenfeld 
    Hi Marco,
The SECMAN errors are harmless.  They are because we do not run a
collector daemon in Condor-Cron.

Setting the port to your production Condor's collector port will
eliminate the errors, however it will also publish your condor-cron
schedd to your production Condor.  Instead, you can just set
COLLECTOR_HOST to be empty in your condor_config.  You will see this
error on startup
6/16 14:14:29 ERROR: Unable to find collector info in configuration file!!!

You can safely ignore this and if will only print once when Condor
starts up.  You won't have the repeating "ERROR: SECMAN:2003:TCP
connection" errors.

Thanks,
scot

    Roger/Marco/SPRACE admins,

SPRACE has stopped uploading RSV records for SPRACE and SPRACE-SE to our collector.
We're trying to dig into these problems with a profiler script -- can you run the
following profiler script, send us that tarball, and then restart RSV? Thanks!

Download Profiler:

wget http://vdt.cs.wisc.edu/scot/rsv-profiler
source rsv-profiler
# provide access to tarball to rsv-dev by email or via web

To Restart:

cd $VDT_LOCATION
vdt-control --off osg-rsv condor-cron
vdt-control --on condor-cron osg-rsv

Hopefully that'll resume the RSV  client. To view RSV probe and consumer jobs ...

condor_cron_q

And you can look at:

$VDT_LOCATION/osg-rsv/logs/consumers/*.log
(and   $VDT_LOCATION/osg-rsv/logs/probes/*.log)

to see there are any indications of any errors.

Let me know if you have any questions.

Thanks,
Arvind
