Hi guys,

I'm wondering if we can get this discussion moving? I worry that for as
long as support for the OSG VO stays at its current level, we're saying
that the OSG VO isn't important. We will never *make* it important if
there's no support for it, and we can hardly hold it up to prospective
users as a model of how to get running on the OSG if we can't get sites
to support our own VO.

To get the ball rolling, I'd like to put up a set of straw-man
statements for comment. Please feel free to add relevant people to the
list, but I think we need to move on this. The longer it languishes in
discussion-land the harder it becomes to convince any prospective users
of the OSG that there's any point to being an *actual* user of the OSG.

I propose:

1. All existing sites are asked via the appropriate mailing lists to
support the following VOs at a minimum:
* mis
* gridex
* osg
* Engage

    2. New sites are asked as part of the sign-on procedure to support
the above-listed VOs.
3. New sites are educated properly as to the security advantages of
using VOMS and/or GUMs and the procedures that are in place to
assure sites that trusting VOs to provide authorization of their
members carries more benefits than risks. New sites proposing
per-user authorization should be discouraged actively from doing
so if at all possible. Sites should be encouraged to honor the
VO's requested account mappings where appropriate. Sites which are
forced to require per-user authorization should be asked for a
straightforward procedure for authorization of VO members which
should be advertised on the site's VORS entry.
4. All sites (existing and new) are encouraged to support the full
raft of VOs unless they are fully saturated all the time or have
other external policy reasons why they cannot.
5. VORS produce *two* distinct VO support lists (both available via
GUI and text interfaces) showing separately: sites advertising
support for a VO regardless of actual support; and sites which
pass a separate set of tests designed to indicate whether members
of that VO can actually run jobs.
6. GOC has well-defined procedures for allowing VOs to request and
/helping sites provide/:
1. The correction of technical problems in a VO's use of a site
claiming to support that VO;
2. The provision of support for that VO on a site that has not
previously advertised support.
7. In the particular case of the OSG VO, that GOC act periodically to
enter and follow up on the following deficiencies at sites
claiming to support it (all these deficiencies may be ascertained
(now or soon) from
http://user-support.opensciencegrid.org/site_tests/osg/latest/:;
1. Sites at version 0.6.0 or greater not reporting to ReSS
("ReSS info -> Has data?" green);
2. Basic fork job success ("CE user check / Status" green);
3. GridFTP access to the CE ("CE GridFTP check" all green);
4. srmcp v1 push / pull access on sites allowing outgoing
network access from WN ("srmcp test" all green(*));
5. wget operation  on sites allowing outgoing network access
from WN (no test currently); and
6. globus-url-copy operation  on sites allowing outgoing
network access from WN (no test currently).

(*) Test (4.7) does not check currently that a site supports basic
outgoing network access; it will shortly.

(6) and (7) will be especially important of course in increasing support
for non-sponsoring VOs in general; and the OSG VO in particular.

In addition, I would like to discuss incentives (publicizing sites
supporting many VOS; league tables of non-sponsoring VO and/or
non-physics jobs run on each site, etc) to increase VO support on
hitherto reticent sites.

I am in favor of a meaningful discussion and consensus on what is
eventually decided; but in the short term I would appreciate explanation
/ clarification of why GOC does not wish to proceed at this time with my
requested tickets to sites claiming to support the OSG VO but where I
encounter problems running.

Thanks for your time and help,
Chris.

    Nov 6, 2007 07:36 PM UTC by Chris Green 
    Hi Tom,

Thanks for this,

Please note: I regard it as *very* important that the VORS listing remain one of intent rather than actuality where a discrepancy exists. There is a big difference between a ticket that says,

Hi, it looks like you intended to support my VO but there appears to be a technical problem: could you take a look please?

and

Hi, I have no idea whether you ever intended to support my VO or not, but I'm going to ask you to do so anyway and make it work.

The one has a good chance of being addressed; the other generally does not.

If VORS regresses to being a service that only reports "known good" supporting sites, then the ability for non-sponsoring VOs to get any kind of a foothold on the OSG will be severely limited. I would urge you to consider this when deciding how to proceed. I would also ask that you *do* go ahead with sub-tickets in this case: every one of these sites has said, for some definition of, "said," that they wish to support the OSG VO. The fact that they don't I regard as a technical issue.

Out of 74 currently-registered production OSG sites, only 45 claim to support the OSG VO; and only 20 do so in reality. We can't claim to be open until we support opportunistic users a whole lot better than we do now. I appreciate the help of the Ops group in being pro-active in this matter: if sites are never asked to fix their problems, they will never be fixed.

Thanks,
Chris.
