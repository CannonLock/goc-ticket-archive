Yes, you are still interpreting it wrong.

CA certificates come via pacman or rpm or a tarball. They are in the
form of .0 files. This is distributed by the GOC or VDT depending on
your chosen distribution. The vdt-update-certs does this task.

The CRLs are gotten directly from the CAs. They are in the form of .r0
files. These are not distributed by the GOC or VDT. The fetch-crl does
this task.

If you clarify what the problem you're seeing is, then I can try to
help. For example, which RSV metric goes red?

CA certificate expires --> metric cacert-expiry will go red -- Affects
availability numbers

CRL expires              --> metric crl-expiry will go red -- Does *not*
affect availability numbers
