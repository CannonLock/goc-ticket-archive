    Jun 18, 2009 07:01 PM UTC by Burt Holzman 
    Hi Steven,

You cannot and should not depend on using $HOME on _any_ OSG site's
batch system.  Even if it exists it is strongly discouraged -- this is
what the OSG_WN_TMP areas are for.

It is defined because bash pulls it out of /etc/passwd on the workers.

A wrapper could be written (for condor, anyway) that remaps HOME to
OSG_WN_TMP, but it requires work on the part of the site admins.

- B

    Jun 18, 2009 05:43 PM UTC by Michael Thomas 
    We use NFSLite at Caltech to remove the dependencies on nfs-mounted user
home directories.  You should not assume that you have access to $HOME
at our site.  While $HOME does exist and is writable on the CE itself,
$HOME on the worker nodes may not exist, and may not point to the same
space as $HOME on the CE.
