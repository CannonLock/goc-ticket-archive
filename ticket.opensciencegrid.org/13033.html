    Dec 7, 2012 02:10 AM UTC by Matyas Selmeci 
    Hi Gabriel,

Since I've been unable to replicate the problem, I have no idea which of the many files voms-admin lays down is responsible.
We'll look into it more closely when we have time; I'll keep the JIRA ticket open.
In the meantime, I suggest the following:

First, make backups! Especially make backups of /etc and /var/lib.

Then remove the following packages, if they exist:
voms, voms-server, voms-clients, voms-admin-server, voms-admin-client, voms-mysql-plugin, and osg-voms.
If you do not have any other tomcat webapps running on that host (such as GUMS), you should also remove:
tomcat6*, emi-trustmanager, and emi-trustmanager-tomcat.

Then remove the /etc/voms and /etc/voms-admin directory trees. If you removed tomcat6, also get rid of the /etc/tomcat6, and /var/lib/tomcat6 directory trees.

Then reinstall osg-voms and configure it using the following page as a guide:
https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Documentation/Release3/InstallVoms

Hope this helps. Sorry we can't look at it too closely right now; we're short on manpower at the moment.

-Mat
