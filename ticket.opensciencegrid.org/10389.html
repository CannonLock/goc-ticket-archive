    May 13, 2011 01:35 AM UTC by Soichi Hayashi 
    (CC-ing Rob & Scott due to potential wide spread issue on is4)

After more than 24 hours of monitoring, here is the result
> http://dl.dropbox.com/u/3209692/soichi-bdii-day.png

It looks like is4 (one of BDII instances for is.grid.iu.edu) is suffering from a data drop out issue. I have discovered that the freshness threshold for is4 is set to 10 minutes, however since is4 receives data from our backup server (which then receives data from is1), there is up to a 10 minute delay between when the data is received at is1 and when is4 actually gets to process the data. It is quite possible that is4's time threshold is too short.. Therefore, I have increased the threshold on is4 to be 20 minutes. I will monitor is4 graph for another 24 hours to see if this improves the current situation.

Please note that I am not entirely sure that this is4 drop out issue is the root cause of the issue that you are reporting (or this might be only one of the causes).
