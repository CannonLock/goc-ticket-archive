    Feb 22, 2013 08:54 PM UTC by humensky@.... 
    Hi Gabriele and Chander,

I'm cc'ing Matthew Wood at SLAC, who is also part of CTA-US and now coordinating our MC efforts and so also very interested in access to the GRID.

My understanding (and I'm new to GRID computing, so may have conceptual misunderstandings) is that we need to get certificates from our national authority before we can join the CTA VO (though it's unclear to me still whether we can at that point join the CTA VO or if we need to be members of a US-based VO).

At this point, that's the extent of the problem - there are not currently CTA VO members attempting to use OSG resources.

Since I'm not sure what level of further detail about CTA you're looking for, I'll just describe the science and computing in a few sentences and you can let me know where you need more information. CTA is the "Cherenkov Telescope Array," a next-generation observatory for gamma-ray astronomy for the study of sources emitting photons with energies of ~50 GeV - 100 TeV or higher. The sources of photons with these kinds of energies are some of the most extreme objects in the universe, including supernova remnants, pulsars, supermassive black holes, and potentially dark matter. CTA will rely heavily on Monte Carlo simulations to evaluate the array's performance and create response functions for the instrument, so at this stage most of the GRID computing work being done is the generation and analysis of MC files. You can find more general information about CTA at http://www.cta-observatory.org/ and about the GRID computing at http://lappwiki01.in2p3.fr/CTA-FR/doku.php?id=cta-computing-grid/ (which may require a login).

Thanks,
Brian
