We have looked at our code. The main script that the job runs does clean up the temporary files. Since this happens in the main script, the cleanup will occur no matter if the job completes successfully or of the application being run crashes. The only way the script will not do the cleanup is if the job is killed by your batch system. If the job is killed then, of course, the script will not complete and the files will not be cleaned up. I'm trying to go through our logs and find a job that corresponds to one that left its files. So far I'm only finding jobs that ran successfully (and did clean up). I have to look more. But I'm 99% sure I'll see the job being externally killed. It is possible that our client is trying to remove the job while it is running. That may act like an external kill (probably does). The guy who submits and watches the jobs is away - but I'll try to send him e-mail and see if he tried to remove the jobs while they ran.
