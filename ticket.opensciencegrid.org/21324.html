    Jun 12, 2014 04:42 PM UTC by Anthony Tiradani 
    Hi Manoj,

glideinWMS is a bit complicated in that respect.  I'll try to give the short version.  There are multiple factories.  In theory, they all serve up the same scripts.  However, to be sure which script is being executed, look at the executable for the job.  It should be glidein_startup.sh.  If you look at the arguments for the script, you will see buried in the middle soemwhere, something like:

-web http://osg-glidein-ce-2.t2.ucsd.edu/glidefactory/stage/glidein_v3_0

So now you can do a curl call or wget call to retrieve 'http://osg-glidein-ce-2.t2.ucsd.edu/glidefactory/stage/glidein_v3_0/glidein_startup.sh';.

However, this won't help you much.  Any and all failures result in error code 1.  The output of the script is written to _condor_stderr and _condor_stdout in the working directory.  These two files are usually sent back to the factory.   In this case they are not.  This is why the factory operators cannot help you very much.  I think the first step is to determine why the output of the jobs is not being transfered back to the factory, or you might have to catch a failing job in the act.  The good news is that even on failure (unless the script fails to execute before it finishes it's setup), there is a built in sleep so that glideinWMS doesn't cycle jobs at a detrimental rate.

Thanks,
