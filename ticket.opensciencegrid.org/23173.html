    Nope, that's just the init script itself. The only things that get automatically started are what exists in /etc/rc.d/rc*.d/ (for the various runlevels).

"man chkconfig" probably explains it in detail, but I think chkconfig just places symlinks in those rc*.d directories to the various init scripts. Without the symlinks, the service never gets started.

Also, I'm guessing this can be closed out now unless there are other issues remaining with hcc-crabserver.unl.edu.
