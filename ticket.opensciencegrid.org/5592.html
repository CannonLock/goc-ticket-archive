Yes.. When you are using condor-g your condor-g client launches
something called the gahp_server, which runs in the allocated
condor port range.  On the FermiGrid machines we set
condor HIGHPORT and LOWPORT  (65535 and 60000 respectively) and then
make sure the firewall is open to those ports.  the gahp_server
is needed if you are transferring executables and stdout/stderr.
If you don't set highport/lowport the gahp_server could end up on any
port.  There is one of these forked per distinct x509 proxy that is
submitting from the client at any given time, so you can't force it
to run on a single port.

The firewall page is somewhat out of date in the twiki.  It should
probably be updated.  I only became aware that the gahp_server
wasn't included in the globus_tcp_source_range / globus_tcp_port_range
set of ports a couple of months ago.

    Hi Steven,

I spoke to Alain Roy about this (CCed on this note), and he thinks it's likely a
situation where your client's firewall is not allowing call-backs from Globus on the
gatekeeper. For that purpose, the twiki document you're looking at is indeed
relevant. And perhaps you've already setup the Globus TCP port ranges on the client
appropriately?

I don't think Condor-G needs any specific ports itself -- it talks via Globus, and
the ports that Globus uses.

Another thing to check would be outgoing firewall rules on the CE (if that's
applicable -- as Alain pointed out to me, most servers allow outgoing connections
without firewall rules).

The last thing Alain mentioned is TCP wrappers -- is it possible those could be
blocking traffic on the gatekeeper ports (2119 for pre-WS and 9443 for WS GRAM, if I
am not mistaken)???

here's another document that might be relevant to you:

https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/ReleaseDocumentation/ComputeElementPostInstall#Included_topic_Compute_Element_F
