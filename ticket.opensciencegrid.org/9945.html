Rob,

I am trying to figure out if it's possible to update the VO name used inside edg-mkgridmap.conf in order to fix the missing-supported-VO issues on MyOSG that Steve has reported.

For your information, following is the chain of information flow.

MyOSG uses list of VOs reported by a RSV probe called "vo-supported-probe"
vo-supported-probe reads VO list from /opt/osg-ce/monitoring/osg-supported-vo-list.txt
osg-supported-vo-list.txt is generated by /opt/osg-ce/osg-vo-map/sbin/check-vo-map which reads /opt/osg-ce/monitoring/osg-user-vo-map.txt and finding account-vo mapping where account actually exists on the CE
osg-user-vo-map.txt is generated by /opt/osg-ce/osg-vo-map/sbin/generate-vo-map which reads /opt/osg-ce/edg/etc/edg-mkgridmap.conf
edg-mkgridmap.conf is updated by vdt-updater via vo-package (but the original one comes from vdt?)
vo-package is maintained by GOC by hand

So, "atlas" VO name ultimately comes from edg-mkgridmap.conf edited at GOC that looks like this

# USER-VO-MAP atlas ATLAS -- 10 -- (usatlas-support-l@....)
group vomss://voms.cern.ch:8443/voms/atlas/ usatlas1
group vomss://voms.cern.ch:8443/voms/atlas/?/atlas/usatlas usatlas3
group vomss://voms.cern.ch:8443/voms/atlas/ usatlas4

The "atlas" in the comment line is the ultimate source of the VO name.

My question is... what will happen if one day GOC updates this comment line to

# USER-VO-MAP usatlas ATLAS -- 10 -- (usatlas-support-l@....)

.. and OSG users update their vo-package. Will it cause a cascading issues on their installation? Will USATLAS VO people still able to submit jobs? What are the side effects? How can I test?
