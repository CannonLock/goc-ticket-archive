On Jun 9, 2006, at 10:15 AM, Marty Humphrey wrote:

> How's this? Brad's currently at CERN and he was able to give me
> something
> pretty quickly...
>
> The University of Virginia CMS group is working on the
> PbWO4 electromagnetic calorimeter and the forward pixel detector
> (FPIX) for
> CMS.  Specifically, for the electromagnetic calorimeter, the UVa
> group is
> preparing a "Supercrystal Tester" for the CMS PbWO4 endcaps,
> investigating
> ways to increase light yield to the vacuum photo triodes (VPT's) of
> the
> endcap to enhance their stability, and finally studying radiation
> damage
> effects on the longitudinal light yield of the Russian and Chinese
> crystals
> using the University of Virginia 600 Curie "gamma-knife" C060
> source. The
> UVa task for the Forward Pixel detector involves preparing two
> "burn-in"
> boxes which will thermally cycle the electronics of the FPIX to
> weed out the
> weak elements.
>
> Other activities include an involvement in developing the new software
> framework (CMSSW) for the CMS analysis system.  We are using our
> experience
> from the Fermilab KTeV experiment in "porting" and revising the
> routines
> that parameterize the electromagetic shower shapes.  We will
> eventually help
> develop the photon "object" for the code.  We are also involved in the
> verification of the GEANT simulations of the EM calorimeter.
>
> We are presently also working with the ECAL group at CERN in the H2
> and
> H4 test beams for the joint HCAL-ECAL test this summer.
>
> Our initial resource that we're planning to use for CMS in OSG is our
> "sunfire" cluster, which is 22 node, dual 2.8GHz Xeon. The queuing
> system is
> SGE. Once successful in getting the CMS simulations running on this
> cluster,
> we will expand UVa's resource participation.
>
> -- Marty
