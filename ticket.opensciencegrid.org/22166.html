    Aug 26, 2014 07:33 PM UTC by Suchandra Thapa 
    Hi David,

There's a little bit of a mismatch between your workflow and what we provide on OSG Connect.  The ideal job for OSG Connect follows the distributed high-thoroughput computing paradigm, e.g. lots of jobs that can be run independently.  Also since the resources on on OSG Connect are used opportunistically, we tend to recommend jobs limit themselves to 2 hour chunks of work since longer jobs may end up getting removed from the compute node in order to allow a high priority job (e.g. a job run by the owner of the cluster) to run.

However, despite that, it looks like we should be able get your workflow running with a bit of work.  It looks like gromacs has checkpointing features so that we can have jobs checkpoint themselves and then restart on another node periodically.  Likewise we do have job slots with multiple cores (up to 8 cores) available that can be used.  However these slots would probably take longer to become available so we would probably need to look at balancing job slot availability vs. number of cores. On the other hand, you could run multiple instances of the simulation at the same time (depending on the number of cores your jobs require) with potentially on the order of a thousand or more single core job slots available and we have users that have obtained 20,000+ cpu-hours in a day without problems.  So the resources are there, it's just a matter of figuring out the best way to structure your workflow to effectively utilize them.

Perhaps, we should have a phone conference to discuss options and alternatives for your workflows and figure out the best way to get you started and running on OSG Connect?
