    Aug 14, 2009 03:45 PM UTC by edgars.znots@.... 
    Hi,
sorry for the long delay in replay, I was on a short vacation at last.

What specific information about the project would you like to know?
The general info is on www.balticgrid.org, in short - BalticGrid is
EGEE-like project, but only for region of Baltic States (Estonia,
Latvia, Lithuania), Poland, Belarus, and is supervised by partners
from CERN and Sweden (KTH). Currently we are in second phase
(BalticGrid Project - II). During our project we have several
activities regarding interoperability between different middlewares
(gLite, ARC, UNICORE), so as well as trying out ARC and UNICORE I was
interested to try out OSG/VDT tools, since two years ago I went to
International Summer School for Grid Computing, where OSG and Condor
were presented, and since Condor ran so well during those courses, VDT
sounded promising and exciting to try out. :)

Additional information regarding my work within "Institute of
Mathematics and Computer Science, University of Latvia" (IMCS UL) - I
am a site administrator for around three years, and I have also
participated in porting applications to BalticGrid gLite
infrastructure. I am also one of the administrators for our national
Grid CA (CALG - "Certification Authority for Latvian Grid"):
http://grid.lumii.lv/section/show/37

In case you need to get confirmation about my status at IMCS UL, you
can also contact Baiba Kaskina (baiba.kaskina@....) or Katrina
Sataki (katrina.sataki@....), they are official representatives
of IMCS UL (SigmaNet laboratory) in the BalticGrid project.

Best regards,
Edgars Znots
