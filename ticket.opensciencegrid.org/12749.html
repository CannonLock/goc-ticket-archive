    Oct 3, 2012 04:47 PM UTC by Mine Altunay 
    I received a reply from David Groep, IGTF EUGridPMA Chair, copied below. David is basically saying that GermanGrid CA issued the certificates for only testing purposes as IGTF had recommended. If the GGUS request is for a change in production environment, OSG should discuss this at GDB.

..."Actually, the IGTF recommends to CAs not to issue SHA-2 certs as
general availability, but to *do make them available* for testing. The
wording of the IGTF recommendation is very precise for this very purpose.
This now is a test of and by GGUS to test SHA-2 compatibility and uses
specially requested SHA-2 certs of the GermanGrid CA to do that. Such
tests are encouraged, and CAs are supporting these tests by making SHA-2
certs available on request.
The GermanGrid CA is compliant with this recommendation by making SHA-2
certs available now to the GGUS team for testing.

As was raised by Maarten already in the GDB and with the the GGUS team
the GGUS alarm tickets have an important impact, and that these (useful!)
tests should thus be conducted in a controlled manner and initially
for the alarm tests. However, once the tests succeed there is no
reason to move back.
Most alarm ticket signatures are processed by humans (all modern email
readers support SHA-2), or are not processed at all (most cases), and
inly in a few instances are the signatures checked by automated tools.
If you run such an automated signature validation tool, having GGUS
test tickets with SHA-2 signatures now is probably a Good Thing.

I think through the GDB you can suggest to the GGUS people to not do the
regular 'production' alarms with the SHA-2 cert just yet.

But there nothing to ask of the GermanGrid CA, who is just doing its
proper recommended task according to the guidelines and time line.
You will be seeing more SHA-2 certs issued on request for various
(testing) purposes by many CAs over the next months, and SHA-2
certs being issued to communities having specifically requested that
SHA-2 certs be issued exclusively to them.

Cheers,
DavidG."
