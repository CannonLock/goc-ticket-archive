Summary: FootPrints Ticket Description:

Thank you for registering the UCSD glidein Resource. By completing the resource registration form you have started the approval process to have your resource added to the OSG Resource Structure.

As a registered OSG resource you should have a representative attend the weekly OSG Operations Meeting to review issues facing your site and the OSG community.

To become an OSG resource and be added to the central infrastructure you should attend the next OSG Operations Meeting. At this time you will be asked to introduce your resource and describe your capabilities to the OSG Community. At this time you will also be asked to comment on any issues your resource has had during installation, configuration, or startup.

The GOC will also do some preliminary testing of the resource and bring any resulting issues to the meeting.

The meetings are every Monday at 1:00pm EST. Please attend this meeting following your registration to allow prompt addition of your resource to OSG.

The TWiki page for this meeting is: http://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Operations/ProductionMeetingMinutes

There are several mailing lists associated with the OSG, as a new Resource you will be subscribed to the following mailing lists:

osg@....  -- For general OSG announcements

osg-general@....  -- For general technical discussion and open community support

osg-sites@.... -- OSG Resource Admins Discussion

osg-operations@....  -- Operations activities

osg-ops-status@....  -- OSG Status Reports

Details for subscribing to these lists and a list of other OSG mailing lists you may be interested in subscribing to can be found at http://www.opensciencegrid.org/?pid=1000089.

    
Please contact the Grid Operations Center (GOC) at goc@.... if you have any questions.

Thank you for registering your resource, and we look forward to your participation in the OSG!

    Jeffrey
In OIM, there is a hierarchy--resource->resource group->site->Facility.
Support centers are associated with Sites--i.e. there are two Sites within
Fermi National Accelerator Facility, the CMS Tier 1 managed by USCMS support
center and FermiGrid which is managed by the Fermilab support center.

Keith--the ticket got routed to us because the destination VO is "MIS"
and FermiGrid Support is the catch-all of all unknown or not-specified VO's.
But the question is why did this ticket come to Fermilab at all?  Should have
stayed in footprints and never come to us.  Neither Fermilab or USCMS support
center is involved with this registration.

I think the USCMS support center was brought in because in the new registration
Jeffery entered "UCSD CMS Tier 2" as the Site when he registered the resource.
Anything at UCSD that is not supported directly by CMS support should be
registered as part of a new Site.

And we are going to close out this ServiceNow ticket on our end because CMS Tier 1 SC
and Fermilab SC have nothing to do with it at all.

STeve Timm
