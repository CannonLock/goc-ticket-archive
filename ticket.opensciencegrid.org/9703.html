    Dec 13, 2010 03:35 PM UTC by GLUEX 
    Igor and all,

I can report major progress in resolving the issues raised in this ticket.  The primary issue was not with my changing the pilot proxy, but with a misconfiguration of my pool collector and the corresponding configuration in my frontend daemon.  Before closing this ticket, I would like to confirm successful completion of a 1000 job cluster using the corrected configuration.  Over the weekend, tests with 100 job clusters have been successful, but I didn't want to try the 1000 job cluster until the experts were present to help resolve any issues they cause.

Problem diagnosis:
On my pool collector node I had configured 1 primary collector (port 9618) and 5 secondary collectors (ports 9620-9624) when I did the initial install.  Either this was the default, or else it was the value used in the sample install session on the install twiki page, one or the other.  However, in my frontend.xml file I had only configured the primary collector, and no secondaries.  Therefore the secondaries were sitting idle, and the primary collector had to do all of the work.  This was ok when I only submitted 10 jobs at a time, but when I submitted a 1000-job cluster the primary collector could not keep up with the load, and it became essentially unresponsive as soon as the pilots started up on remote sites and started registering.

Problem resolution:
I stopped glidecondor on the collector node, went into the condor_config file and replicated the sections where the secondary collectors COLLECTOR0 - COLLECTOR4 were defined, extending the list to COLLECTOR0-COLLECTOR99.  I then restarted glidecondor and confirmed that the secondary collectors were all up and listening on ports 9620-9719.  I opened these ports in the firewall for incoming connections from anywhere.  Then I shut down the frontend daemon, edited the frontend.xml file to add secondary collectors on ports 9620-9719, did a frontend reconfig and then restart.  After this, I tested the configuration by launching a 100-job cluster.  Pilots from ucsd.edu and uconn.edu began to register almost immediately.  After tweaking the condor submit file to include a line for x509userproxy, the ucsd.edu pilots started accepting my jobs, and ran successfully, writing the results back to my SE at UConn.

Further tests:
I would like to try this test again with a 1000-job cluster, as soon as Igor gives me the green light.  I would then like to have the destination sites for Gluex pilots enlarged somewhat and try the test again.  I would like to have demo jobs running successfully under glideinWMS on all of the sites that are configured to accept Gluex jobs before closing this ticket, if that is ok.

    [by Richard T. Jones]

I had an initial success in getting Gluex jobs to run on the UConn-OSG site using the glideinWMS, with help from Igor Svigloi at UCSD.  After that, he made a change to enable my jobs to flow to other sites.  Initially all other sites were refusing the pilots because the proxy/FQAN I was using was not configured in the standard GUMS template that most sites are using.  That was not great because the pilots were wasting cycles sitting one worker nodes at various sites.  During the weekly VO call, it was suggested that I switch the pilot proxy/FQAN to one that is enabled in the current GUMS template, and try again.  I did not change any of the certs used to authenticate communication between the frontend and the factory, only the proxies that get shipped out with the jobs.

After making this change, it seems that I am causing major havoc at other sites, with my jobs flowing out to many sites and clogging up the queues with pilots that cannot connect back to my pool.  Furthermore, my pool Collector is getting a flood of hundreds of failed connection attempts from Startd's on remote sites, complaining that the Startd's on the other end are hanging up the connection (see CollectorLog snippet below).  The flood of these failed connections quickly saturates my collector, so that even "condor_status" on the local machine times out.  I suppose that most of the remote pilots are also reporting that my Collector is not answering the phone, just because it is overloaded with these failures.

The major concern I have is that I am causing disruption at major sites who have agreed to accept my jobs, and I have no control over what I am doing.  At this point I will discontinue use of the glideinWMS mechanism for the Gluex VO until I have some confidence that this is not going to happen again.  Site admins who have been surprised by this intrusion on their site should feel free to bar Gluex from future use on your site, with my apologies!  We are still able to run jobs by sending them to our own site gatekeeper, so we will continue to function in that way, for the time being.

Suggestions for alternatives to glideinWMS are welcome.
