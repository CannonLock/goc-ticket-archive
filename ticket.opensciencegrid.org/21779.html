    Hello Richard,
the certinfo files accumulating in  /var/lib/gratia/data are caused by a previous bug that was solved in the previous release.
You should have no more the problem but the files will clear slowly (in up to a month). You can accelerate the process and clear by hand the older leftover files.

Here the steps:
- move the history_* files from /var/lib/gratia/data/quarantine to /var/lib/gratia/data/
- wait for the probe to run (few minutes)
If they go back to quarantine please attach to this ticket one of the files and your configuration
- in any case you can remove all the files /var/lib/gratia/data/gratia_certinfo_condor_* that are older than 4 days to accelerate the removal of leftover files.

    Jul 17, 2014 03:40 PM UTC by echism 
    There has been some confusion with this issue, not the least of which being that this issue
resembles an earlier, similar bug. We apologize. Here are the details including a workaround
and permanent solution.

* Who is affected?
Sites with a job manager other than HTCondor are *not* affected and can ignore this.
The bug affects the Gratia HTCondor probe from version 1.13.24 to 1.13.29 (current
production version) and flags all your jobs as Local (non grid jobs).

*  Severity: MILD to SEVERE
- If you kept the default probe configuration (i.e. you *did not* manually edit
/etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig), then MILD,  you are reporting correct numbers to WLCG (if
enabled). If you check your /etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig and see a line
SuppressGridLocalRecords="0", your level is MILD, your jobs are misclassified but few people
look at that level of detail.
- If you changed the probe not to report local jobs (i.e. you edited
/etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig and changed to SuppressGridLocalRecords="1"), then
SEVERE, please take action URGENTLY to apply the workaround below. Without it you are not
reporting correctly to OSG and WLCG.

* What you should do:
If you have a HTCondor job manager (very likely if you received this notification), evaluate
your severity level (point above).
- If the severity is MILD: you may apply the workaround if you like (please see below) or
wait for the new production release next Tuesday (22/Jul).
- If the severity is SEVERE: apply the workaround (below) as soon as possible and contact
Gratia support (open a GOC ticket or email gratia-operation@....) for
instructions to recover the jobs that have not been reported.

* Workaround
Edit the probe configuration (/etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig) and add the line:
NoCertinfoBatchRecordsAreLocal="0"
It can be anywhere in the ProbeConfiguration element, e.g. right below the line
EnableProbe="1".
This workaround will make your reports correct: Grid jobs are correctly classified as Grid jobs

    and reported correctly to WLCG
Local jobs are not reported as you desired

* Solution
gratia-probe-common.noarch 1.13.30-3 and gratia-probe-condor.noarch 1.13.30-3 are
currently in osg-testing and will be part of an urgent release next Tuesday 7/22.
Everyone (using the HTCondor Gratia probe) is recommended to upgrade to that release.
The upgrade will involve a regular update of the package (yum update) and a merging of the
new configuration file (/etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig.rpmnew) with your current one
(/etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig):
- this will add the line NoCertinfoBatchRecordsAreLocal="0" to your configuration file
- if you applied the workaround above you can simply delete the
/etc/gratia/condor/ProbeConfig.rpmnew after the yum update

* Additional details
The bug is discussed and tracked in JIRA, https://jira.opensciencegrid.org/browse/GRATIA-
149
