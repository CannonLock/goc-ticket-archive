These are DNs for Services and not for People. As long as the service,
which can be a client, is running on the named host (glidein-1.t2.ucsd.edu)
then it meets the DOEGrids policy.  If the long term private key of those
certificates is being used a a host other than that named it would be
a policy violation.
Doug

In investigating the processes that are running on the head node on antaeus and
causing it to slow down, we find that these are running under certificates assigned
apparently not to individuals, as required by IGTF rules, but to subject names that
are generic glide-ins running from UCSD.

Unless I misunderstand the circumstances for issuance of these certificates, this is
an explicit violation of the terms of the CP/CPS of DOEGrids, which require that
certificates be issued with subject names that are tied to real human individuals. At
minimum, we need to understand this and also why these processes are so
resource-intensive on our cluster head node at this time.

Please let me know if I have misunderstood anything.

These are potentially problematic in terms of subject DN:

Account 'uscmspool2011' is mapped to grid identity(s):
/DC=org/DC=doegrids/OU=Services/CN=uscmspilot46/glidein-1.t2.ucsd.edu

Account 'uscmspool2015' is mapped to grid identity(s):
/DC=org/DC=doegrids/OU=Services/CN=uscmspilot49/glidein-1.t2.ucsd.edu

These may be OK, as this is the certificate set used for SAM tests:

Account 'uscmspool0365' is mapped to grid identity(s):
/DC=ch/DC=cern/OU=Organic Units/OU=Users/CN=sciaba/CN=430796/CN=Andrea Sciaba

Account 'uscmspool0846' is mapped to grid identity(s):

    /DC=ch/DC=cern/OU=Organic Units/OU=Users/CN=asciaba/CN=430796/CN=Andrea Sciaba

Please advise.

Alan
