and then stop again ... leads me to believe that.

Can you also do the following after creating the above tarballs --

Edit $VDT_LOCATION/post-install/osg-rsv, and replace the following lines:

su -c "$CONDOR_EXE_SUBMIT $file" $RUN_AS_USER > /dev/null 2>&1

with

su -c ".  $VDT_LOCATION/vdt/etc/condor-devel-env.sh; $CONDOR_EXE_SUBMIT $file"
$RUN_AS_USER

Then restart RSV by doing:
vdt-control --off osg-rsv condor-devel

    vdt-control --on condor-devel osg-rsv

The most likely solution is a simple restart of the OSG-RSV service by doing the
following:

cd <path/to/CE>    ## this cd'ing is important to get around a bug with the RSV init
script
. setup.sh
vdt-control --off osg-rsv condor-devel
vdt-control --on condor-devel osg-rsv

If the resource is not using condor-devel (ie it's using a production Condor > 6.9.x)
then you'll just have to restart osg-rsv.
