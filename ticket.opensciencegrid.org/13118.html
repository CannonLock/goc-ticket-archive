    Hi Mike,

Let me try to take a stab at this.

In order for a job to run at your site, the DN of the incoming job must
be authenticated by either GUMS or gridmap at your site. This is just
what you would expect. Perhaps what is not so expected is that glidein
jobs do not enter your site using the DN of one of your local
users. Glidein jobs authenticate using the DN belonging to one of the
many pilot DNs. I am not exactly sure, but I can image that there are
100 different glidein pilot DNs. Once the glidein job starts at a site,
it runs a private version of condor and joins the glidein center's
condor pool. At this point, jobs from the glidein server are started at
a site via condor and they never go through X509 authentication, i.e.,
GUMS and gridmap are out of the picture.  What this means is that by
having a gridmap file with only the DNs of your local users, you will
prevent any and all glidein jobs from landing at your site. The only way
that you can "whitelist" your local users would be to have them submit
their jobs using the glite scheduler. If I am not mistaken, that
scheduler is supposed to go away at some point.

So in my opinion, you are effectively blacklisting all glidein jobs and
whitelisting your local users only if they use glite. When I check:

http://dashb-cms-jobsmry.cern.ch/dashboard/request.py/dailysummary

http://myosg.grid.iu.edu/rgaccount/index?summary_attrs_showservice=on&summary_attrs_showrsvstatus=on&summary_attrs_showfqdn=on&gip_status_attrs_showtestresults=on&downtime_attrs_showpast=&account_type=daily_hours_byusername&ce_account_type=gip_vo&se_account_type=vo_transfer_volume&bdiitree_type=total_jobs&bdii_object=service&bdii_server=is-osg&start_type=7daysago&start_date=04%2F27%2F2013&end_type=now&end_date=04%2F27%2F2013&facility_10009=on&rg=on&rg_264=on&gridtype=on&gridtype_1=on&active=on&active_value=1&disable_value=1

I don't see any jobs listed as having run at Baylor and the Gratia plot
indicates that the only user running there is Andrea Sciaba who runs the
SUM tests. So I would say that nothing is running at Baylor.

I know of no way to whitelist a user in the glidein system, well at
least not gracefully. This would have to happen at the glidein
server. While I doubt that the glidein software was written with this

    feature in mind, I am not 100% sure. Someone else would have to answer
that question.

I also think that whitelisting users via DNs is potentially disruptive
to other glite users. If CRAB matches non-Baylor jobs to your site, I
believe those jobs will fail and the user will have to resubmit the jobs
making sure to blacklist Baylor. I doubt that this is a big effect, so
perhaps you needn't worry about it. Perhaps someone else can also
clarify this detail.

Hope this helps,

    Mar 18, 2013 10:56 PM UTC by doug johnson 
    Hi Mike,

You do not have allow all CMS users direct access to the cluster to
allow glideIn jobs, but glideIn jobs provide a "backdoor" for any CMS
user to end up on your system. I imagine that there are of order 50-100
glideIn DNs (purely a guess), so you would have to allow those on your
system.

There is a potential negative side effect by restricting DNs.  When a
glideIn job runs, it acts a conduit for a "real" CMS job to run. There
are no restrictions as to who that CMS user might be. The side effect is
that if you use glexec (recommended), the real job will fail if that
user is not permitted on your site. If you simply reject a glideIn job
then no "real" fails.

I might also add that the Colorado system is shared between CMS grid
jobs and about 4 other HEP groups. I have configured the batch system so
that when the system is full there will be at least 400 CMS MC jobs
running, 300 locally submitted jobs running and then the rest is run via
fair share queuing. We almost never have anyone complain about not
getting their share. When someone isn't running, someone else is able to
use the system. I favor keeping the system as busy as possible yet
providing a facility for people to get their fair share.

Hope this helps,
doug
