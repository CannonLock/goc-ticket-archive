Ignore Vanderbilt (for now). The issue I'm trying to report is that there are simply *zero* transfers between most pairs of sites and have been for quite a long time. It also happens for Vanderbilt, but let me point out another example

https://cmsfts3.fnal.gov:8449/fts3/ftsmon/#/?vo=&source_se=srm:;%2F%2Fmaite.iihe.ac.be&dest_se=&time_window=6

Not a single transfer to IIHE was attempted in the last 6 hours, even though there are >5000 files in the queue.

This link has 100% success rate over the last 12 hours:

https://cmsfts3.fnal.gov:8449/fts3/ftsmon/#/?vo=&source_se=srm:;%2F%2Fheplnx204.pp.rl.ac.uk&dest_se=srm:%2F%2Fcit-se.ultralight.org&time_window=12

but the autotuner has repeatedly punished the link between the site for bad efficiency ... even though not a single file has been moved for a good 10 hours or so:

https://cmsfts3.fnal.gov:8449/fts3/ftsmon/#/optimizer/detailed?source=srm:;%2F%2Fheplnx204.pp.rl.ac.uk&destination=srm:%2F%2Fcit-se.ultralight.org&time_window=12

... and before that, transfers were working fine, the autotuner reporting "Good link efficiency, current average throughput is larger than the preceding average" then suddenly deciding to drain the queue and not submit any more jobs to the site.

Hope that makes a bit more sense.

Now, RE the specific transfer you found -- there's an even distribution of errors on the hosts behind the loadbalancer, so I don't suspect it's faulty hardware. It might have something to do with the fact that the file is 28GB. I'll run some transfers by hand and see what happens.
