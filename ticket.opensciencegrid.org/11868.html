    Mar 26, 2012 01:56 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    Dear all,
ggus.org uses at the moment a certificate within the following hierarchy (which is not part of IGTF):
subject= /C=DE/O=Deutsche Telekom AG/OU=T-TeleSec Trust Center/CN=Deutsche Telekom Root CA 2
subject= /C=DE/O=DFN-Verein/OU=DFN-PKI/CN=DFN-Verein PCA Global - G01
subject= /C=DE/ST=Baden-Wuerttemberg/L=Karlsruhe/O=Karlsruhe Institute of Technology/OU=Steinbuch Centre for Computing/CN=KIT-CA/

This CA was used to get rid of the error messages at the client side, because the Telekom root CA is distributed within the browser. Browsers normally don't know the Grid-CA's (IGTF-releases) which confuses simple users.
Unfortunately now there appears a problem at the other side.

There are several possibilities: Your side accepts this CA hierarchy although it is not in the IGTF release.
(certificates to download at our KIT-CA: https://pki.pca.dfn.de/kit-ca/pub/cacert/chain.txt )
More Information (sorry mainly German):
http://www.telesec.de/pki/roots.html
https://www.pki.dfn.de/root/globalroot/

So you may decide whether to trust this CA or not. If not there is the other possibility to switch back and use a GridKa-CA certificate, which is accredited within EuGridPMA/IGTF.

Hope I could help a little bit.
Ursula Epting

http://www.telesec.de/pki/roots.html
