    Do you mean "access" in terms of using the space reservation, or having the authorization to release it? For the former, there is no checking now: Timur
has answered,

The space manager only performs access control  for the reservation requests. Once the space reservation
is made, anyone who knows the space reservation id will be able to write into it.

But for releasing a reservation, the role of the user is checked against the role that made the reservation. The mapping to username does not come into
play at all, so no way outside of the proxy to manipulate it. Only the first role in the proxy is considered. It's conceivable that if there were a special space
reservation making role which was always first in the proxy, users could add additional different roles as needed for other operations. Might be kind of
awkward, though.

Hi Everyone,

I have a question about configuring dCache for space-tokens.
Basically the issue seems to be that a space-token is associated

    with a specific VO and Role, e.g., /atlas/Role=production.

Here is our current setup at AGLT2:

http://head02.aglt2.org/dcache_spresv.html

What I want to be able to do is to assign a user account to the
space-token.   That would immediately allow multiple Roles to access
the same space token.   For example, /atlas/Role=production and /
atlas/usatlas/Role=production both are mapped by GUMS/VOMS to
'usatlas1'.

https://hep.pa.msu.edu/twiki/bin/view/AGLT2/SettingUpATLASHOTDISK

"forced" by directly modifying the postgres DB with SQL.

rather than just VO/Role?  Is there another way to achieve this?
