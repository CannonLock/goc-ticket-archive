Summary*        : Gratia seems to be working correctly. See explanation.
Notes           : Hi Ian,

I'm sorry things are not working optimally for you at the moment.

Examining the individual records pertaining to this user's jobs as submitted to SBGrid-Harvard-Exp shows that his jobs are being executed as the user, "jkong." rather than (for instance) at WQCG-Harvard-OSG where his jobs are running as the user "nebiogrid."

Also, the probe is appending a note, "GratiaJobOrigin not GRAM" indicating that the SBGrid jobs are not coming through GRAM. Indeed, the jobs are labeled with the unusual job universe number 9. This indicates to me that your user is submitting his grid jobs from the SBGrid-Harvard-Exp gatekeeper to run elsewhere via CondorG, rather than from a client. This will create a local condor schedd entry for each job at the SBGrid-Harvard-Exp gatekeeper even though the job is actually getting shipped off for execution at a grid site (which may or may not be the same site -- this is irrelevant). These local entries will be processed by Gratia, labeled, "local" and uploaded unless you tell the probe not to.

There are two possible solutions here: one (followed eg at FNAL) is that users submit to local clients rather than the gatekeeper and do not generally have login privileges to the gatekeeper. The other is that the probe be set to not upload local jobs with the ProbeConfig item:
SuppressGridLocalRecords="1"

As best I can tell, your gatekeeper, Gratia system and osg-user-vo-map.txt file are behaving correctly for the use pattern the gatekeeper is seeing. It is up to you to decide how best to deal with what is essentially a double counting problem.

Let me know if you have further questions, comments or problems.

Thanks,
Chris.
