Hi all-

We recently ran out of space in dCache. As it turns out, ~45% of our
space was used by files that had been removed from PNFS but remained on
our pool disks. We've observed this problem in the past, but never at
such a high frequency.

For example, the following file was removed this morning by PhEDEx:

/pnfs/hep.wisc.edu/data4/PA_TFC/store/Generators/2008/4/13/FastSim-QCD_250_500-madgraph-
1208074320/0001/A2B297BA-FC09-DD11-9A81-000423D6B2D8.root

Indeed, it no longer exists in our PNFS. Before it was removed, we found
three replicas of this file on our various pools (this is expected).
After the removal, even though the file no longer exists in PNFS, I find
it on each of the three pools. One of the pools hosting replicas for
this file (000600000000000005D9C0E0) is g10n20_0:
