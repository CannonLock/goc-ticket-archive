    Jan 8, 2013 12:42 PM UTC by Don Krieger 
    I've attached a PNG showing the past 24 hours of Condor mediated jobs and have a couple of thoughts.
I've cc'd Rob Quick and Mats Rynge in case the attachment doesn't make it into the ticket log.

You can see break during which the osg-xsede.grid.iu.edu frontend was down with a bunch of my jobs on both sides, both running and queued but idle.
I restarted my spawner just a few minutes after the reboot.

The sawtooth look of the tracing after the reboot is what is routinely seen; this is due to a governor in my script which prevents process spawning if the number of jobs in condor_q which belong to me is larger than a set limit.  I have been using 10,000 but raised that several times to a maximum of 17,000 during this run.  Once the governor is released by the #jobs falling below the limit, typically 3000 jobs are spawned irrespective of the total.

The sharp spike in #jobs in the queue at about 22:00 was, I presume, due to user dcm.

Notice that before the break, the system is showing no governing for the most part.  This has been noticeable for a couple of weeks and I had seen several things which suggested that there might be a problem with the scratch file system.  I still think that that may have caused the crash, perhaps overrunning some table due to excess file fragmentation perhaps.  There may be a clue if there is a log of the file recovery actions during the reboot this AM.

In any case since the reboot, the system is clearly behaving differently and more as it had been several weeks ago.

Thanks again for all of your efforts.  If there's anything I can do to help, I'm happy to do so.

Regards,

Don
