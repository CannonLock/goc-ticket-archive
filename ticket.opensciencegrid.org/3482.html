>>> On Mon, 7 May 2007, David Meyers wrote:

>>>> Hi Steve:
>>>>
>>>> After your explanation of the computing model surrounding
>>>> FNAL_FERMIGRID, I realize we are probably not planning the workflow

>>>> properly for where the jobs actually end up at FNAL.
>>>>
>>>> The Pegasus planner typically generates all the condor_submit
>>>> scripts at planning time before the scripts are submitted,  with a
>>>> particular site in mind, say OSG_LIGO_PSU or UWMilwaukee.
>>>> The assumption that we know the location of $OSG_DATA etc in
>>>> advance and can write out the condor_submit scripts by knowing the
>>>> final location where the jobs will run is violated in the computing

>>>> model employed with FNAL_FERMIGRID.
>>>>
>>>> We will need to have a late binding mechanism, that defers part of
>>>> the path planning until execution time, given that FNAL_FERMIGRID
>>>> apparently can't currently negotiate that information back to the
>>>> process that is setting up a workflow.   Are there any plans for
job
>>>> reservations on a particular cluster within the FNAL_FERMIGRID
>>>> domain ? It may be that the current methodology employed in the
>>>> Pegasus planner is inadequate for the style of late binding that
>>>> the FNAL_FERMIGRID computing model requires.
>>>>
>>>> I will ask the Pegasus team to comment on this further.
>>>>
>>>> David

Description of Problem:
I am filing this ticket on behalf of Britta Daudert,
a new LIGO user who is trying to run the NanoHIPE
workflow of LIGO on FNAL_FERMIGRID.
(Her E-mail is copied).
Up until now she
has been able to run on one of our subclusters,
FNAL_GPFARM but not the main site.
I have met with her in person and several times
via phone and E-mail.

So far all I have been able to determine is that
the jobs, which all have as their
executable /usr/local/grid/vds/bin/kickstart,
are exiting immediately through no fault of grid
or condor.  Progress is being slowed due to
the fact that GRATIA is cleaning up the logs
every ten minutes--therefore every time
we try to work this problem we have
to set up a time in advance, disable the crons,
etc., and progress has been quite slow.
There's no usable stdout or stderr from the
job which is failing, which is the 5th stage and

    onward of a very complex DAG.

I suspect that there are unintended consequences
that occur when the fermigrid job forwarder picks
up /usr/local/grid/vds/bin/kickstart and sends
it on its way to the sub-clusters in question,
but don't know enough about VDS to be sure.

Obviously the LIGO science milestone is very important
and should prove both a worthy test of this new
campaign and an appropriate one. FermiGrid wants
to give LIGO all the support we can to help work
this out.
