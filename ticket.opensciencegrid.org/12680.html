    Sep 17, 2012 07:32 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    [by Richard T. Jones]

I have noticed over the past few months a common feature that jobs from certain VO's that we accept (but do not belong to) land on our site and start running, then get preempted by a local user and are never able to run after that.  The reason is that while the job was running, its resident set or disk footprint on the worker node grew outside the resource allocation for that node.  Condor updated the job's internal attributes with the latest numbers before it was kicked off, and after that, it cannot start again because the RequestMemory or RequestDisk is now too large to meet the condor requirements for our site.  The result is that the job sits forever idle in our queue.  I used to periodically run a script to reset the job attributes to 0 so the jobs would finish, but now I see that sometimes the user proxy has run out and the job spins indefinitely.  I am now finding this too much work, and am resorting just to killing (condor_rm) any glidein that is idle and unstartable on our site, or whose user proxy has expired.  Is this consistent with OSG site behavior or best practices?  Should I be contacting the VO's and reminding them to read our Site Usage statement (http://gryphn.phys.uconn.edu/UConn-OSG) ?  This can also be time-consuming.  On the other hand, I don't want to cause confusion at the glidein factories, whose glideins are going stale, then mysteriously disappearing on our site after a certain period of time.
