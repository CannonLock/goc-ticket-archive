    Chris,

So according to your reply, "if the Resource Short Name changes, then the SiteName needs to be
changed *and* Gratia-Ops needs to be notified to change the reporting." I'll update our internal
procedures accordingly. DNS changes are not to be sent to you from what I understand. Also, as a
side note, you should use "Resource Short Name" instead of "SiteName" to comply with the OSG
Information Management (OIM) guidelines. That terminology is the terminology of OIM, which is
the official information management source for OSG. More will be published about this in the
coming months, but you can be ahead of many others if you get started now. Site refers to a
physical location that may be host to many VO's and Resources (the actual nodes).

Thanks again,

Tim
    Nov 7, 2007 09:51 PM UTC by Chris Green 
    Not quite: the gratia probe sends two pieces of information: the
MeterName, which is the name assigned to the probe and encodes the type
of probe and the DNS name, by convention; and the SiteName, which is the
Resource Short Name in your terminology. If the DNS name changes, only
the MeterName need be changed in the local ProbeConfig; if the Resource
Short Name changes, then the SiteName needs to be changed *and*
Gratia-Ops needs to be notified to change the reporting.

Thanks,
Chris.

    Nov 7, 2007 02:51 PM UTC by Chris Green 
    Morning, Tim,

We already get copies of registration forms to gratia-operation. DNS
changes, IP changes and the like are transparent to us: in the case of a
node name change the site should (for reasons of convention) change
their MeterName in ProbeConfig (configure-osg.sh should do that,
though). The only change that requires manual intervention at our end is
the SiteName for the reason that we need to preserve and report on a
site consistently across a SiteName change boundary. If you wanted,
maybe a way to make sure we don't miss these is to flag a resource name
change form differently than a new resource registration form, and list
the old and new names in same.

Thanks for thinking in the general rather than just the specific; I
think we're pretty close in terms of desired efficiency already, but
above suggestion might reduce the chance of missing a cue at our end.

Thanks,
Chris.
