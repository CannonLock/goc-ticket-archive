    Feb 24, 2011 04:47 PM UTC by GLUEX 
    Steven,

If at all possible, you should avoid running your job directly in an nfs directory, but rather set up a local copy of your job environment in the local disk directory set up by condor for your job.  We provide read-only access to $OSG_APP and $OSG_DATA to make it easy to do.  This local directory is pointed to by the environment variable $OSG_WN_TMP and is owned by the userid that your job is running under.  As stated under item #3 on our site's Usage Policy web page <http://zeus.phys.uconn.edu/UConn-OSG>;:

Jobs must be able to run within a small local filesystem on the worker nodes pointed to by the environment variable $OSG_WN_TMP, which has about 10GB of local storage capacity. This includes all files that are locally staged during job startup, plus whatever output and log files are generated by the job during execution. Jobs that overflow this limit should consider how to make use of space in the $OSG_APP and $OSG_DATA areas (see the Storage section below), and also the local dCache Storage Element.

Not sure if you have ever tried to run a site with more than a couple hundred cores, but if you don't do something to restrict nfs traffic, jobs trying to do direct io over nfs will quickly swamp the network. NFS is apparently much less efficient than a streaming protocol like gridftp.

-Richard J.

    Feb 24, 2011 11:35 AM UTC by GLUEX 
    Steven,

I looked over this script, and I believe the problem is the following.  You are assuming that your job has locally mounted $OSG_DATA with write access on the worker node.  I am using the vdt's condor-nfslite distribution of condor on my site, and direct nfs i/o from worker nodes is restricted.  Jobs have read-only access to ~nanohub, $OSG_DATA and $OSG_APP from the worker nodes.  These are nfs mounted, and our site policy has read-only access on the worker nodes to these areas.  To write to these areas, you need to use globus-url-copy with a destination like gsiftp://ce1.phys.uconn.edu/$OSG_DATA/nanohub/xxx

-Richard J.
