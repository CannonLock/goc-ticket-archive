    Nov 30, 2006 08:51 PM UTC by Horst Severini 
    Hi Kevin,

I ran some more tests, and I agree with John and Bockjoo that it's a
hostname issue, since 'hostname' and 'hostname -i' report
'vmpg01.vampire' and '10.0.0.7', which is the internal information, rather
than the public one, 'vampire.accre.vanderbilt.edu' and '129.59.197.240'.

By the way, why doesn't even the short hostname match between the public
and the private network? That seems very confusing.

So I agree with Bockjoo, you should fix the gatekeeper's hostname
configuration to report the external FQHN and IP address rather than
the internal one, and that should fix everything.

In the mean time, you can try the following, which may or may not work.

In /etc/xinetd.d/globus-gatekeeper, add

env         += GLOBUS_HOSTNAME=vampire.accre.vanderbilt.edu

In globus/etc/globus-user-env.[c]sh, add

export GLOBUS_HOSTNAME=vampire.accre.vanderbilt.edu (or setenv ...)

and in globus/etc/globus-job-manager.conf, add

-globus-gatekeeper-host vampire.accre.vanderbilt.edu

Hopefully that will fix it.

But it would be much better and cleaner if the hostname info was changed
on the gatekeeper directly. The current situation is very confusing,
and I would claim just plain wrong, since not even the short hostname match.

And I don't think the entry in /etc/hosts will help much, since that's

    not always consulted.

Good luck,

Horst
