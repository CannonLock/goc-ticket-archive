    This condition is detected by the Gratia-APEL interface software that runs daily and summarizes Tier1/2 CMS/ATLAS/ALICE Gratia activity for a month and sends it to APEL-EGI for monthly MOU reporting.   As a proactive measure, it generates this email if a Gratia site/resource has had nothing reported to Gratia for more than 2 days during the month and are NOT inactive or NOT down for maintenance as reported in OIM.  The full context of the email and not just what was reported here is attached.  If not readable, please advise.  Tickets generated for this interfaces emails should provide some context other than coming from a "gratia report".

The interface is described here: https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/bin/view/Accounting/GratiaInterfacesApelLcg

In this particular case, this site was activated mid-month.  Since there is no time-sensitivity to most anything in OIM, there is no way of knowing that non-reporting days prior to 8/18 were not a problem.  Generally, tickets generated from the Gratia-APEL email would provide some context and should have been a separate ticket to be addressed by the respective Tier 2 coordinator.

One simple means of eliminating this message that comes out daily (so long as the condition exists) would have been to mark the period from 8/1 thru 8/18 as down for maintenance (which fortunately is time-sensitive)  Otherwise, it is just ignored and the ticket noted that this site went live mid-month.

Since this site/resource is now reporting from when it actually went live, it is a non-issue.  It does require a judgement call on someone's part.

As for the heartbeat, it is not really an indication of a site's ability to report to Gratia.  Just one aspect of it.  There are still inactive/disabled resources sending heartbeats.

This and the activation of this resource/site as a glideinwms factory entry point  should have been separate tickets.
