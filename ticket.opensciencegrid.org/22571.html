Dear T1 network admins,

This message intends to inform you
about a new Vidyo router available

on the

CERN LHC Vidyo network
at this address: 196.24.243.2

(vidcern01.tenet.ac.za).

I
would therefore ask you please to update your firewall configuration
accordingly in order to avoid disruptions on the service operation.
Thanks for your confirmation.

To recall:

There are
basically 2 security perspectives: the VidyoDesktop client
perspective and the VidyoRouter one, that in this case applies also to
you

since you are hosting routers.

Summarizing:

1. If
you have users using Vidyo from your site the ports to their
Desktops/Mobiles are the following:
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Clients to Portal and VidyoManager (vidyoportal.cern.ch):

- TCP
80 and 443 (for browsing to portal)

- TCP 17992

Clients
to VidyoRouters (please see list of vidyorouters below):

- TCP
Port 17990 (SCIP) and UDP Ports 50000-65535 (RTP/sRTP/RTCP)
bidirectional to the Vidyo routers

Clients to Vidyo Router
Proxy Connections (see list of vidyorouters

below):

- TCP
443 (if TCP 17990 is closed and the UDP range is closed the

client
needs VidyoProxy to be forced. If TCP 17990/17992 is open and UDP is
closed

the system will bounce the Desktop client to VidyoProxy
automatically)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Then, if you are hosting a vidyorouter server (that is your case in
fact),

these ports needs to be set from your vidyorouter (to all
vidyorouters on

all the CERN Vidyo Network and
vidyoportal.cern.ch, when it

applies):
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
VidyoRouter to VidyoRouter for cascading (see the list of vidyorouters
below):

- TCP 17990 (VidyoRouter SCIP signaling to and from
VidyoDesktop

clients and

between routers)

- UDP 50000-
65535 (media between VidyoDesktop clients and other

routers)
VidyoRouter(s) to/from VidyoManager (vidyoportal.cern.ch):

-
TCP 17991 (RMCP signaling between each Router and VM)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The list of routers is here:

http://information-
technology.web.cern.ch/services/fe/info/vidyo-

routers-cernlhc-
vidyo-network

The Vidyoportal is sitting at vidyoportal.cern.ch
Thank you very much in advance for your collaboration.

Cheers,
Joao

CERN IT

Dear T1 network admins,

This message intends to inform you about a new Vidyo router available on the
CERN LHC Vidyo network at this address: 196.24.243.2 (vidcern01.tenet.ac.za).

I would therefore ask you please to update your firewall configuration
accordingly in order to avoid disruptions on the service operation.
Thanks for your confirmation.

To recall:

There are basically 2 security perspectives: the VidyoDesktop client
perspective and the VidyoRouter one, that in this case applies also to you
since you are hosting routers.

Summarizing:

1. If you have users using Vidyo from your site the ports to their
Desktops/Mobiles are the following:

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Clients to Portal and VidyoManager (vidyoportal.cern.ch):

- TCP 80 and 443 (for browsing to portal)

- TCP 17992

Clients to VidyoRouters (please see list of vidyorouters below):

- TCP Port 17990 (SCIP) and UDP Ports 50000-65535 (RTP/sRTP/RTCP)
bidirectional to the Vidyo routers

Clients to Vidyo Router Proxy Connections (see list of vidyorouters below):

    
- TCP 443 (if TCP 17990 is closed and the UDP range is closed the client
needs VidyoProxy to be forced. If TCP 17990/17992 is open and UDP is closed
the system will bounce the Desktop client to VidyoProxy automatically)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Then, if you are hosting a vidyorouter server (that is your case in fact),
these ports needs to be set from your vidyorouter (to all vidyorouters on
all the CERN Vidyo Network and vidyoportal.cern.ch, when it applies):

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
VidyoRouter to VidyoRouter for cascading (see the list of vidyorouters below):

- TCP 17990 (VidyoRouter SCIP signaling to and from VidyoDesktop clients and
between routers)

- UDP 50000-65535 (media between VidyoDesktop clients and other routers)

VidyoRouter(s) to/from VidyoManager (vidyoportal.cern.ch):

- TCP 17991 (RMCP signaling between each Router and VM)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

The list of routers is here:

http://information-technology.web.cern.ch/services/fe/info/vidyo-routers-cernlhc-vidyo-network
