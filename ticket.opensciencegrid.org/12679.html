    Hi All;

I am also CCing Shawn McKee so he is aware of this interaction.

Vanderbilt is provided services by the SOX regional network.  The 'proper' R&E path to travel to get to Universidad de Los Andes should go something like the following:

Vanderbilt -> SOX -> Internet2 or NLR -> AMPATH/AMLIGHT -> RedClara -> Universidad de Los Andes

As an FYI FIU, UF, and AMPATH do take the correct route.  The Vanderbilt route is different than the SOX route (in terms of specific path) but it it still commodity.

For those unaccustomed to this data - the 'localpref' values are used to decide on the route to take, in this case 'higher' is better.  There are several options in place in terms of route:

- The first two are SOX Dallas (143.215.194.130), and these have the highest localpref.  What is disturbing is this AS path:

>> 23520 19429 19429 19429 19429 19429 3603

That basically has the same entry repeated 5 times to ensure its taken.  This is the 'COLUMBUS-NETWORKS' path as seen in the trace route.

1) The third is a RedClara peering (143.215.193.26), this would be the most direct path and probably should be preferred.
2) The fourth is NLR (and then to RedClara)
3) The last is Internet2 (and then to RedClara)

- To confirm what we see above, we can also look at the SOX Dallas router via proxy

> Response From Juniper MX240 Router
> ...............................> show route 157.253.0.0/16
>
> inet.0: 21657 destinations, 41659 routes (21407 active, 0 holddown, 499 hidden)
> + = Active Route, - = Last Active, * = Both
>
> 157.253.0.0/16     *[BGP/170] 01:01:24, localpref 100
>                       AS path: 23520 19429 19429 19429 19429 19429 3603 I
>                     > to 198.32.124.179 via xe-1/0/0.839
>                     [BGP/170] 01:01:24, localpref 100
>                       AS path: 23520 19429 19429 19429 19429 19429 3603 I
>                     > to 198.32.124.208 via xe-1/0/0.839

This has the single route to 'COLUMBUS-NETWORKS'.

My summary is that Internet2 does have the route available, and SOX is learning it from us as well as from other sources (NLR, RedClara directly, and commodity) but for some reason has made a change to prefer the commodity route.  This could be for some internal need (e.g. SOX may have a specific reason to do this).  What is most likely happening is that since Vanderbilt learns from SOX, they are presented with two options:

- The SOX route which is artificially being given a high preference and travels over commodity
- The Commodity route they see via QWEST (connectivity that is purchased outside of SOX) which appears to be 'better'

At this point the correct next step is for ACCRE (I am assuming thats John M) to bring this to the attention of the local IT group at Vanderbilt.  If Vanderbilt only takes the R&E routes from SOX as is (I believe this is the case), then ACCRE/Vanderbilt IT will need to work with SOX to find a solution to this problem.  Internet2 can't really assist on these matters directly unfortunately.

Hope this helps, if you do require any more assistance please let me know.

thanks;
