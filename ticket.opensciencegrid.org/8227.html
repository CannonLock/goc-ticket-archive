    Mar 24, 2010 08:25 PM UTC by Arvind Gopu 
    Added Dave Ritchie to the ticket's CC so there is no more a need to cc him in email responses. [For completeness: right now, Ruth is the customer on the ticket; Rob, Soichi, and I are on it as GOC staff; and Brian B and Dave R are cc'ed on the ticket]

I had a wide-ranging conversation with Dave. I will try to summarize below; Dave, please feel free to put in any updates and/or correct me if I mis-speak.

1) Ruth/Dave (and other management folks) usually grab a screen shot of the MyOSG status and/or "Sites in the US" maps for presentations they do.

Status map: http://myosg.grid.iu.edu/map?all_sites=on&active=on&active_value=1&disable_value=1&gridtype=on&gridtype_1=on
Sites in US with shortened names: http://myosg.grid.iu.edu/map/promo?acro

Ruth would like it to be easier to transfer these map images to a power point presentation or some such.

(a) I suggested she use Fireshot add-on if she uses the Firefox browser. It lets one to capture screen shots, edit them, crop them, etc. [https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/5648] ... once you have an image you like, you could just add it to your powerpoint.

(b) Dave tried another similar tool named "Snipper" on his Mac or is it Windows VIsta computer?).

Dave indicated he will be willing to show Ruth how to use these tools.

2) I remembered something about the status map being US-centric. I believe this is intentional, and may have been a request from someone in the OSG management; the status map allows one to zoom out to view European/AFrican/Asian sites.

3) I showed around MyOSG, OIM, etc. to Dave per his request

4) At this point the discussion veered to a completely different topic unrelated to this ticket; I will try to summarize quickly, and we could have further discussion as necessary in another ticket or email thread.
-- Dave pointed out MyOSG is mostly technical with very little people integration; He mentioned a website named GridGuide (http://www.gridguide.org/) ... it has some nice integration of technical details with people information, multimedia, etc.
-- We both agreed that the way that website was done (according to Dave - information filled out by one or few people about everyone) is not scalable, and a better approach would be to ask OSG members to add information to their profile on OIM (For example)
-- Dave mentioned integrating to web entries from ISGTW, etc. We could consider such requests as long as data transfer standards, format, etc. can be worked out AND if there is enough interest in such an addition.
-- I suggested that this topic is still a bit vague; Dave said he was still formulating what he had in mind
--- We both agreed it would be best to chat further in the future ... probably when we all meet face-to-face in a month or two.
--- Dave might email us some information in the interim.

Cheers,
