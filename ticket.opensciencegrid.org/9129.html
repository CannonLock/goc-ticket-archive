    Aug 24, 2010 06:14 PM UTC by Soichi Hayashi 
    Marco,

I totally agree with you, that this is very strange.

I don't remember who said what and when, but someone has said that, we can not update confirmation date when user clicks submit button automatically since most users come to OIM to update specific field - they rarely check everything on the page. Therefore, resource update time should not always be the resource information confirmation date.

Please let me know if you don't agree with this and I will bring it up to the management.

Also, we used to have the confirmation button outside of the form so that whenever user clicks the button, it will update the confirmation date independent of the rest of the form. I can't recall exactly why this was a problem, but basically having 2 separate forms on the same page was causing technical issues within the application as well as user issue where some users were confused about the button and forgetting to update the actual form after they've changed resource content.

So, we've settle on what we have now - with the warning label "Please click following button and submit the form to update the confirmation date." Most likely, "confirming" the form will require some kind of updates to the information on the form, and it will be a sure way for user to submit the form after making such changes with less confusion about what button does what.

What are your thoughts?

Thanks,
Soichi
