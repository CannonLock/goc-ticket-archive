    Hi Kevin,

First, https://vampire.accre.vanderbilt.edu:8443/rsv/ shows you as critical for your SE (se1.accre.vanderbilt.edu).

Second, in MyOSG, we do show why a metric is critical if we have that information. In this case though MyOSG says "SRMCP Read / Write (Not Reported) " i.e it thinks the metric was never reported back to us.

The MyOSG URL you sent us is for the resource named "Vanderbilt", which only provides a CE, and does not provide an SE. Is this correct?

You should instead look at resource group "Vanderbilt" -- http://myosg.grid.iu.edu/wizardcurrentstatus/index?datasource=currentstatus¤t_status_attrs_shownc=on&gip_status_attrs_showtestresults=on&account_type=cumulative_hours&ce_account_type=gip_vo&se_account_type=vo_transfer_volume&start_type=7daysago&start_date=06%2F12%2F2009&end_type=now&end_date=06%2F12%2F2009&rg=on&rg_137=on&r_137=on&active=on&active_value=1&disable_value=1

This shows all the resources, and services they provide in that resource_group. Unfortunately this looks identical to the link you provided because your OIM registration is not accurate (partly because our interface is likely not intuitive, and needs to be tweaked). We consider a resource to be a computer with a hostname which provides services like CE/SRM/etc. In your case se1 should be registered as a separate resource but ...

You appear to have sandwiched your SRM service registration into the resource "Vanderbilt" (FQDN: vampire.accre).

1) You should instead register a new resource under the "Vanderbilt" resource group, say Vanderbilt_SE (FQDN: se1.accre), and map the SRMv2 service on it ... do enter the non-standard port information (but I would skip the service_name part (/srmv2)).
2) Finally remove the SRM mapping from your vampire.accre resource registration.

End result:
Resource_group: Vanderbilt
Resource: Vanderbilt (vampire) with service: CE (and gridFtp if applicable)
Resource: Vanderbilt_SE (se01) with service: SRM (and gridFtp if applicable)

If this is all too confusing then let me know, and one of us will be happy to call you on the phone and walk you through it.
