    Ok, I found the reason for the failures, although the error message is much more dramatic, it was just a permissions problem, that at least the namenode logs show it explicitly. After the upgrade directories were being created with the 022 umask, in the past it was enough to enforce umask 002 in Bestman (and that didn't change), but after I made sure that fuse configurations and hadoop clients were all 022 (5 hours ago) the situation improved a lot. I see some errors here and there but the main permissions one doesn't seem to come back.

The fact that only new directories (after the upgrade) were created with the wrong umask, explain why sites started failing one by one.

I think that we can close the ticket.

Thanks,
Samir

Dear all,

I just upgraded to hadoop 2.0.0+545-1.cdh4.1.1.p0.20.osg32.el6.x86_64
and apparently started having transfer issues in another timestamp :

https://cmsweb.cern.ch/phedex/debug/Activity:;:QualityPlots?graph=quality_all&entity=src&src_filter=&dest_filter=T2_US_Caltech&no_mss=true&period=l48h&upto=&.submit=Update

What is puzzling is that in the beginning transfers to Nebraska and
MIT actually worked, then started failing.

I tried to reproduce the problem by hand, with lcg-cp but it worked
just fine[1]. To emulate PhEDEx transfers even better I submitted an
FTS job (another analog one for MIT) :

https://www-ftsmon.gridpp.rl.ac.uk:8449/fts3/ftsmon/#/job/c107be7e-976f-4ec8-9aad-9c5874770c20

Both surprisingly succeeded. This morning I realized that the
"GLOBUS_THREAD_MODEL=pthread" configuration was still in the sysconfig
file from some tests, and removed it in the hope to improve things.
didn't help a lot.

The same error message from FTS is what I see in the GridFTP logs, but
it doesn't give a lot of clues where of where to look further. Have

    you ran into this before?

Another puzzling part of the problem is that it's from MIT,Nebraska
and IN2P3 only, according to the most frequent errors in :
