    Feb 1, 2010 07:14 PM UTC by Ian Stokes-Rees 
    Alex,

Are you familiar with anyone at CCS at BU? http://ccs.bu.edu/  They host
an Open Science Grid site and may be the best BU-local contact to get
OSG access.  Saul Youssef is a Physics professor at BU who is very
involved with this.  At the same time, it may be that the OSG activities
at CCS are exclusively for the physics community, or that they will
provide you with OSG access, but only limited support.

If you decide to go outside of BU for OSG access, we can set you up with
an account, a digital certificate, and direct support measured at the
level of 0.25 to 0.75 days per week over a period of several months to
get you started running jobs on OSG.  We are based at Harvard Medical
School and have specific funding from OSG to support life sciences
researchers in New England in their use of OSG.

We will need some details about the computing you are trying to do:
computational load, applications, data volumes, etc.

To get started with an account, you will need to complete the
application here:

http://abitibi.sbgrid.org/grid/register/wizard

You will need to fax us the completed form from step 1 along with a copy
of your BU photo ID.

There is a bug in step 2.  Skip it if it does not work properly for you.

For the rest, please read the small print for details.  Email or give us
a call (details on every page) if you have any questions along the way.

As an enticement, we have been running around 50,000 CPU hours per day.
This is about 10 years of computing every day.  For certain problems,
this allows you to get a lot of computing done.  Of course, you then

    have to deal with all the resulting data.

Regards,

Ian
