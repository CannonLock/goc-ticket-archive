    May 20, 2016 09:33 PM UTC 
    Hi,

You've got most of it. When I was looking at your gums.config file I did notice that you already have a bunch of entries for the lbne VO in there. Since the LBNE VO is being retired in favor of the DUNE VO, we can re-use some of the LBNE stuff to minimize the changes.

1) In the vomsServers section of the gums config, find the entry for the lbne server and replace "lbne" with "dune" for the whole entry.

2) In the userGroups section, find the lbne entries. You can replace those with a single entry that looks like this (very similar to the fermilab entry):

<vomsUserGroup
name='dune'
access='read self'
description=''
vomsServer='dune'
matchFQAN='vo'
acceptProxyWithoutFQAN='false'
voGroup='/dune'/>

3) In the accountMappers section of the config file you'll also find several groupAccountMapper entries for various lbne accounts. If you do not already have a dune local account created, you can leave the lbne entry in place. Otherwise replace lbne with dune.

4) Here is the only remotely tricky part. In the groupToAccountMappings section you will find a groupToAccountMapping entry for lbne (you can just worry about the "lbne" entry and forget about lbneana, lbnepro, etc. for now.) In that entry, change all cases of lbne to dune, *except* for the accountMappers='lbne' line. If you do not have any local dune accounts created, leave that line as lbne. If you do have local dune accounts, it is OK to replace it. As I understand it all this does is control what local account the jobs will run under after glexec. Also, if you don't have a local dune account right now but create one later, then you can modify the accountMappers line at that point.

5) In the hostToGroupMappings section, there is a line like "groupToAccountMappings='cdf-fnal, ... ". Simply add dune to that list.

6) Restart gums and knock on wood, we will be in good shape.

Many thanks,

Ken
