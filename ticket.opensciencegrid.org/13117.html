    Hi John,

It's still a bit confusing to me & it may be that the process has
changed since 2011.  The relative numbers are in agreement but, for
completeness, I'll explain my confusion.

What I thought was happening was that the approximation to convert Si2k
to HS06 was done as: kSi2k * 4 = HS06.   So then, a Si2k value of 2,347
= 2.347 kSi2k --> 9.388 HS06.  The old normalization factor (3.48 in our
case before) was then the core-weighted mean kSi2k value. So that every
NERSC-PDSF cpu-hour was this mean 3.48 kSi2k value times 4 for the mean
cpu-hour of 13.92 HS06.  In comparison, your spread sheet seems to
calculate HS06*1000 as the unit or mHS06.    That seems to be consistent
with my understanding as the per core HS06 values listed on

http://gratiaweb.grid.iu.edu/gratia/wlcg_reporting#gip

are order 10-20 not order 10000-20000.    Whether this is a problem just
depends on how you apply it in the end. I agree with the weighted mean
you calculate is 16544. I just would have called it 16.54 HS06.

I do have one suggestion from your spread sheet regarding the 2nd row,
584 cores at SI2K=4020 for the Xeon X5650 2.67GHz.  The 4020 Si2k value
was taken from an old spread sheet.  When using the 4x scale (as I
describe above), that gives 16.08 ( your 16080).   But there are several
sites with the same cpu listed on the above link, all of which use 13.8
HS06.    I suspect this is more correct given the vintage of this
processor.  So I would change that row by dropping the Si2k values and
adding simply 13800 for column E.  After propagating it from there, you
should end up with a weighted average of 15494.

I think that's the right value for NERSC-PDSF cluster.  The LLNL cluster
hasn't changed so that normalization should be fine.

thanks,

    
Jeff
