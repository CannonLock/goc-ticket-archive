    Apr 26, 2010 07:38 PM UTC by Brian Moe 
    When all nodes are up, there are 1492 slots available.  (1432 w/1GB RAM,
60 w/2GB.  Soon upgrading to 1432 w/1GB, 120 w/2GB = 1552 slots)

We share the entire cluster with OSG users, but local users have a higher
priority, though Condor will not let OSG jobs starve.

Local usage varies considerably, depending on when people have deadlines
or meetings.  Things are quiet right now, but will probably ramp up again
late May, early June then get quiet again late June.

Gratia does have an accurate picture of what is going on, but you need
to look at per-VO utilization.  If you look at the LIGO VO, you see the
situation you describe -- large wallclock times, much smaller CPU times
indicating a LOT of idle time.  But the situation for other VOs is
considerable better.  This is because, perhaps ironically, the LIGO VO
has its priority cranked way down, orders of magnitude lower than other
OSG VOs.  Condor is configured to be considerably kinder to non-LIGO VOs.

If you go back a few months, you will see the LIGO VO getting better
treatment and maybe get an idea of what sort of throughput you might
expect.

Brian
