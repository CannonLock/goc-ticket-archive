    Jun 17, 2016 03:56 PM UTC by Brian Lin 
    I've assigned this to our release manager Tim Theisen but I don't believe he'll be able to get to it today, so I'll try to answer your questions as best as I can. If you haven't taken a look at the contents of the CE and WN spec files, you'll see that they're metapackages that pull in other RPMs so you won't find one overarching source for the CE software stack. We have some of our projects available on Github (https://github.com/opensciencegrid/) like HTCondor-CE, osg-configure, etc. Some of the other packages that are either very simple (osg-version) or are passthroughs of other RPMs (condor, many globus packages) can be found in our SVN (https://vdt.cs.wisc.edu/svn/native/redhat/trunk/).

As for the licensing, I don't know the exact details so I'll have to leave that to Tim but we are the OPEN Science Grid after all, so everything is indeed open :).
