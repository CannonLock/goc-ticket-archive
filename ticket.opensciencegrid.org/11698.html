    May 22, 2012 02:56 PM UTC by antonyat@.... 
    Dear Kyle,

Thank you for your email.

We have diligently tried to get our Xserve cluster online by installing
Linux upon it. We were unsuccessful.

Originally, we opted for a Virtual Machine approach using VirtualBox. By
installing CentOS on the virtual machine ran by the Compute Element,
this would give us the flexibility to easily maintain the server and to
have a co-operative environment between Mac and Linux. Due to networking
restrictions, we were unable to allocate a static and remotely
accessible IP address for the virtual machine. In essence, we could not
assign the Ethernet card's address directly to the virtual machine. This
was attempted via port forwarding but using this method did not address
the hostname resolution issue required by OSG certificates.

To mitigate this issue, we decided to install CentOS directly upon the
Xserve. However, we were un-able to get the Grub boot-loader, modified
to support EFI, to recognize installation media, whether it was a USB
stick or an EFI specific installation disc.

At this time, we will cease our efforts to place APCR-DRDL Xserve
cluster located at Florida A&M University's upon the OSG.

We thank you for your support in this issue and hope that in the future
we can make resources available to Open Science Grid.

Future correspondence on this matter may originate from a different
individual as I have graduated and will be moving on to a full-time
position at Microsoft.

-- Antony Jepson, System Manager
