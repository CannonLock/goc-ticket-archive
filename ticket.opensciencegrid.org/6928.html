    > Today the reports are much closer. I also noticed a new field was added - FQDNAliases.

Glad to hear. I suspect you emailed in saying they didn't match before you got a new report following our central RSV change I emailed you about.

Yes, we add FQDNaliases for resources that go by multiple DNS hostnames, and have registered that detail in OIM. In general, we expect to add new details as necessary or requested by users. We will announce such adds as part of our major release notifications. Our goal is to make additions in a way that does not break existing XML parsers. Anyone using XPATH for example, will not be affected by such a change.

> So now CIT_CMS_T2B, UCSDT2-B show up on VORS but not RSV.

I once again do not understand what you mean by that. They show up on both MyOSG and VORS.

> These are two cases of a resource being in a resource group. Can jobs be submitted to these sites?  At least in some cases they can.

I do not know but I would guess a CE that advertises support for your VO will allow you to run jobs.

> It is not clear how to treat sites that are marked with GIPStatus = UNKNOWN.

This is relevant to users who use BDII (which gets its information GIP). It is a new component, and the GIP developer tells us that a lot of OSG sites have to fix local site GIP configurations to make it completely clean.

> As you mentioned GLOW-ATLAS is not being reported by RSV.

We are looking into this, like I said.

> Clemson-Birdnest has switched from an ITB site in VORS to a production site in RSV. Is this correct?

We pick this information up from OIM, and the OIM registrations are set by the site-admins. My guess is that VORS was not aware of a change the site's OIM registration --- VORS did not sync well with OIM, and required several manual updates ... one of its problems.

> Clemson-IT changed to CLEMSON-IT. A small but not necessarily negligible change.

Why is this not a negligible change? Are you saying your code differentiates between resource names in a case sensitive way?
