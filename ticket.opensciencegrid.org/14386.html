    Freija,

Kyle will be out of the office for the later half of this week, so I am assigning myself to this ticket in his place. In a VO context, the support center is generally the people who support your scientific applications. It is my understanding that you don't need this from OSG and you are only looking for certificates, so you can go ahead and list OSG-GOC (which is us) as your support center, since you don't actually need one.

You will also want to make sure that the following is unchecked:
"This VO has users who do OSG-dependent scientific research."

Although the certificate case is technically OSG-dependent, this statement refers to OSG computing resources.

I would then list yourself as the contacts; you can adjust these later at will. I will do my best to expedite this request when I see it.

Thank you,

    Apr 5, 2013 08:00 PM UTC by echism 
    Hello Freija,

I am thinking you ended up on this page:
https://oim.grid.iu.edu/oim/certificaterequestuser

Is that correct? If so, it is possible that you do not need to do this step. Do you have an x509 certificate that identifies you, such as one you would use to register in the VOMS server you mentioned, or to generate a proxy to run a job? If you do have such a thing, then as long as your certificate authority is a member of the international grid trust federation, you do not need another certificate. All you would need to do would be to load your certificate in your browser or keychain, approach http://oim.grid.iu.edu/oim/home and click the "login" button. You should then be prompted to fill out a profile.

Most people who are new to OSG have many questions about this organization and its requirements and procedures. If this is the case with you, I encourage you to call 1-317-278-9699 and ask for me (Elizabeth) so that I may take the time to explain anything you need to know.

Thank you and welcome,
Elizabeth
