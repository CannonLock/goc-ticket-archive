    Sep 19, 2007 10:57 PM UTC by Chris Green 
    Hi,

For future versions we will instruct the Condor probe to remove GRAM
stubs for jobs for which the information has rotated out of the history
file; for Condor v6.9 with a correctly configured PER_JOB_HISTORY_DIR
however, the problem is moot because the ClassAd is stored immediately
in the correct area by Condor without the need for a GRAM stub as a trigger.

As a solution to the current problem however: once you know that your
probe is reporting current jobs to the Gratia collector you should
remove all old remaining GRAM stub files from the data area.

Thanks, and we apologize that this limitation has caused you problems.

Chris.

Subject: gratia condor probe building up failing job logs
Date-Sent: Monday, September 17, 2007 5:31 PM -0500

I observed that one of our CEs in Wisconsin had about 100,000 job logs in
$OSG_LOCATION/gratia/var/data, many from weeks, and even months ago.  I also saw
similar problems last week on the FNAL CE cmsosgce.

The condor probe logs are full of messages such as this: "Failed to feed XML 004
event to Gratia" and "Failed to feed XML 005 event to Gratia".

Question: does gratia assume that condor does not rotate the job history file?  I see
that gratia is running condor_history commands on jobids that no longer have a record
in condor_history, because they have been rotated off the end of the file some time
ago.  If the failure to find a history record causes the operation to fail and then
be retried later, then job reports will get stuck in this state of failing and
retrying over and over.  I don't know how things could have gotten so far behind that
jobs would be getting rotated off the history before Gratia had a chance to query
them, but I am worried that once it does get into this state, there may be a
never-ending loop.

We are just using the default Condor configuration for history rotation
(MAX_HISTORY_LOG and MAX_HISTORY_ROTATIONS), so at any time, there may be up to 60MB
of job history, but no more.

Thanks,
--Dan
