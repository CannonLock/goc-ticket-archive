Attached is the throughput plot seen from T1_US_FNAL tape storage.
On 2017-04-27, the peak reached 1.8GB/s, which shows what it is capable of delivering.
There are many factors that would affect the staging test throughput, such as,
[1] other activities - the system was not idling, lots of other transfers were going on at the same time. There were quite some write requests (not shown in the picture) on 2017-04-28 which preempted reads
[2] many files may reside on the same tape - readings are serialized due to this - this effect is more pronounced with high density tapes - we use 5TB tapes
The individual transfer rate from tape to dCache pool is about 160MB/s. One newer node (in test configuration) reads at 256MB/s which is the maximal tape drive speed.
