    Hello Elizabeth,

firatly, apologies, as I hadn't heard anything back by way of an
acknowledgment of the issue being raised (although I was not expecting
much more, having already found OSG's opening up of your issue tracking
system's ticket to have been useful and with my cert not being issued
within the OSG's "domain), I had not thought to copy you in to the
developments, as that would have seen morre single emails as opposed
to emails directed to an "issue ticket".

We believe that the issue has been narrowed down to a problem with
a supporting library, traditionally packaged by distributions as
'nss' and/or 'libnss'.

Our ArchLinux Firefox, 24.6, where the orignal PKCS12 certificate
import failed, as well as an earlier FF17, has been shown to fail
against two PkgSrc builds of NSS greater than 3.16.1nb1.

The issue has been demonstrated for 3.16.2 and 3.16.3

The issue has been replicated for a non-PkgSrc google-chrome browser
on the same ArchLinux platform.

Similarly, an Ubuntu 12.04 platform, with FF30 but which has a
libnss3_3.15.4-0ubuntu0.12.04.2_amd64 (3.15.4) completes the import
of the certificate.

On our 64-bit windows 7 terminal server, which has Firefox 31.0, and
where the certificate import also fails, but where an import into an
IE (iexplorer) 11.0 completes, we have seen that the supporting NSS
DLLs, which appeared to be bundled with the FF browser, have version
numbers as follows

nss3.dll     31.0.0.5310
nssckbi.dll   1.98.0.0

    nssdbm3.dll   3.16.2.0

We note the 3.16.2 in the last of those.

Hoping that this information might be useful to you, but, just out of
interest, why the encouragement to top-post in the lines below ?
Kevin

OK, so we have got to the bottom of this for the ArchLinux
platform.

The issue lies with the NSS package, somewhere between

nss-3.16.1nb1

and

nss-3.16.3

If we downgrade to the first of those, then the firefox-24.6 is OK
as is the google-chrome browser.

I could imagine that the FF 31 on the windows platform comes bundled
with a later NSS, but have yet to test that yet.

Glad to have to the bottom of this, and hope that the information
around it is useful - it's a load off my mind.

    
I expect our PkgSrc man will be feeding back the information into the
PkgSrc community.
