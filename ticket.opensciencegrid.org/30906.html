    Aug 10, 2016 06:44 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    Many linux systems, especially those managed by systemd, now use a 'tmpfs' filesystem for /var/run (via a mapping to /run) and /var/lock.  As such, the directories that may be created on installation for htcondor-ce and rsv for /var/{run,lock}/condor-ce and /var/{run,lock}/condor-cron are expunged on reboot and do not exist when these services are restarted on bootup.

There are a couple of ways of dealing with this -- the systemd way is a tmpfiles.d script, however given that both condor-ce and condor-cron are started through scripts in /etc/init.d/ I would recommend the most generic method would be to simply add a couple of lines in their start() section to ensure the needed directories exist and have the necessary user and group ownership.  I have a patch (which hopefully I can attach after submitting this) with the changes I made to support this on my CE.
