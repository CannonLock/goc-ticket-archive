    Jul 1, 2009 04:19 PM UTC by Parag Mhashilkar 
    Hi Prasanna,

You can find documentation on ReSS at
https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/twiki/bin/view/ResourceSelection/WebHome
The publications demonstrate ReSS architecture, its use cases and in
production deployment/application of the service.

Based on your mails, my understanding is that you need means to query
OSG Information Systems to extract useful information and most of the
info you need is available in BDII. Is that correct? If so, you will be
happy to know that almost all the information available in OSG BDII is
also available in ReSS in condor classad format. You will need a condor
client installation (also available through OSG/VDT).

You can simply issue following commands to get this information -

# list of resources
condor_status -pool osg-ress-1.fnal.gov

# Detailed information in condor classad format
condor_status -l -pool osg-ress-1.fnal.gov

# In xml
condor_status -pool osg-ress-1.fnal.gov -xml

You can refer to condor documentation for more details about
condor_status command. If you are interested in a subset of sites that
satisfy your requirements, you can use -constraints option. Constraints
apply on information available in the classads

# Constraint the list to show classads with
GlueCEInfoHostName=="d0cabosg1.fnal.gov"

condor_status -pool osg-ress-1b.fnal.gov -constraint "GlueCEInfoHostName
== \"d0cabosg1.fnal.gov\""

    
I am not sure why you need ReSS source code or its documentation, since
based on your mails, I doubt you will benefit from it. Most of the
questions you have should be addressed in the two publications available
on the ReSS website I mentioned above. As such ReSS is integration
project and uses several off the shelf tools with some
customization/configuration. If you still think you need the source, I
can make some parts that we control, available to you.

Feel free to ask any questions you have.
