    Jan 25, 2010 08:11 PM UTC by Scott Kronenfeld 
    First question:
You probably had some Gratia scripts that were unable to be delivered
many days ago.  Since then, the logs have been rotated.  The error
message at the top of the HTML page is new, and it's to help admins
identify that there is a problem.

To remove it:
Look in $VDT_LOCATION/osg-rsv/output/failed-gratia-scripts

You probably have 6 files in there.  Manually move all of them into
$VDT_LOCATION/osg-rsv/output/gratia/

They will be sent next time the gratia consumer runs, which is every 5
minutes or so.  If they can't be sent again, you will have a message
in the indicated log file.

The error at the top of the HTML page will go away when the HTML
consumer runs (it runs about every 5 minutes) and the
failed-gratia-scripts directory is empty.

I'll look into your second question now.

Thanks,
scot

    Hi Dave,
I think I've figured out the problem.

After updating, did you run this script to fetch the latest RSV probes?
$VDT_LOCATION/osg-rsv/setup/update_from_svn.sh

If you followed the 1.2.5 instructions, you probably didn't do this,
since it wasn't listed.  It inadvertently got dropped between the
1.2.4 and 1.2.5 update instructions.

Here's what you need to do now.

cd VDT_LOCATION
. setup.sh
vdt-control --off osg-rsv
$VDT_LOCATION/osg-rsv/setup/update_from_svn.sh
configure-osg -c -m RSV
vdt-control --on osg-rsv

I think that will fix both the "usage" message, and the page not
updating frequently enough.  Let me know how it goes.

We're working on re-packaging RSV to get rid of this extra step, so in
future updates it won't be necessary.

Thanks,
scot
