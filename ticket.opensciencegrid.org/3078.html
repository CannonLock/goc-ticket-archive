New Work Log Entry : Manual update:

updated on 02/14/2007 at 16:21:16 with the following information:

Hello Steve and all who have followed up on this ticket,

Through the ticket responses, I have been pointed to several sites for documentation, but those are the sites I had already visited before sending in my ticket - and I hadn't found the information I was seeking there.  I was hoping for general information on the resources and their specifications (OS, number CPUs, compilers, batch scheduler info, mpi info and paths, etc, etc); this is information useful for a new user to OSG and somewhat different from the monitoring information which is in fact fairly readily available on your sites.  (The monitoring info is very valuable, of course, but it is not the same thing as a concrete description of the resources which is also very valuable.)  I see that some sites have their own informative pages about their resources, but I did not find such pages for Fermi.

I spoke yesterday with David Meyers, who gave me a lot of helpful information and who will doubtless be a continuing source of information in the future.  With his experience on OSG, he was able to give me some good advice for starting out: 90% of the OSG resources are Intel linux clusters, compiling locally and exporting the executable is usually the soundest approach, most systems are 32-bit and the migration over to 64-bit is beginning now, etc, etc.  In addition, Chris Green from Fermi spent some days last week here at RENCI, also giving us the benefit of his experience and getting us going at Fermi.  So I am actually accumulating some of the information I was seeking.  The voice of experience is quite helpful to starting out, so some of the tips could possibly be collected into a bootstrapping guide for OSG.

Anyway, as long as the descriptive documentation for new users to OSG does not exist, there is no point in keeping this ticket open - what I am looking for simply is not there.  On the other hand, I have very much appreciated the helpfulness of the experienced users and the assurance that some of this documentation will be forthcoming as OSG moves along.

Thanks very much,
Leesa

New Work Log Entry : The following was e-mailed to the Requester:

Dear Leesa

The home page that describes grid resources at Fermilab
is http://fermigrid.fnal.gov

FNAL_FERMIGRID is our main OSG site and that is
a site gateway which forwards to five subclusters
on the Fermilab site.  You can see some details about
them in the various monitoring pages we have up.

Since it is a site gateway some of the fields in
the various OSG monitoring facilities such as
GridCat and MonALISA may not always show up correctly
but we invite you to look through the various documentation
we have on the site.

One thing is that to submit any job to our site
you need to use a

voms-proxy-init

type proxy otherwise we will reject your job.

If you have further questions, feel free to follow up.

Steve Timm
