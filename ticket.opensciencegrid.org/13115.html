    Terrence,

Very nice.  I see that OSG_SITE_READ/WRITE point to that store, but I was not sure if a special agreement is required for guest VO's to get space on your SE, so I didn't immediately start pushing to it.   Here is how the Gluex VO would like to use /hadoop/oppo, if you would agree.  We need a shared area where workers can read static (or rarely changing) tables for configuring a physics simulation: magnetic field maps, geant4 data tables, channel-detector readout maps, and the like.  The total volume for the foreseeable future will be less than 50GB. Dragging these tables over the internet from our VO server to every worker node at startup seems like a waste, and also annoys our university netops who notice the traffic.  We had been using OSG_APP for these tables until recently when the volume grew above a few GB and we decided that most sites do not size OSG_APP for these purposes, and switched to OSG_DATA.

When you say, "streaming semantics" do you mean gsiftp/srm protocols, or do you also support an application fstream layer with certain restrictions, something like dcap in the dcache world?

-Richard J.

    OSG_DATA is not supported at UCSD anymore.

If you need local site storage for data there is a hadoop area that is
writable from the CE and readable from the worker nodes. /hadoop/oppo.
However it does not have full file system semantics and only supports
streaming writes not random writes.

We can work with you to try to find a solution to allowing storage of
your data at UCSD, including an area in hadoop that is only writable by
your VO.

Terrence
