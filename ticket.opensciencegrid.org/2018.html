> <plug>
> Welcome to the Open Science Grid.  Please visit our site at https://
> www.opensciencegrid.org.  Fermilab is a founding member of the OSG,
> and one of our staff is the executive director, Ruth Pordes.
> Intense efforts have been made to make OSG interoperable with LCG
> and vice versa.
> </plug>
>
> The fermilab VO is hosted, obviously, at Fermilab, specifically at
> https://voms.fnal.gov:8443.  This VOMS server has been in
> production for over a year.  In the fermilab VO exist the following
> groups, corresponding to experiments or divisions that reside at
> Fermilab:
>
> accelerator
> astro
> cdms
> hypercp
> ktev
> miniboone
> minos
> mipp

    > numi
> patriot
> theory
> nova
>
> There are currently ~4600 registered members of the fermilab VO
> corresponding to people with Fermilab IDs.  Chances are if you have
> a Fermilab ID you're in the fermilab VO although you may not be in
> a specific group, yet.  To request membership in the minos group
> send mail to the group manager, Liz Buckley-Geer, buckley@.....
>
> To get VOMS extended attributes with minos permissions, please add
> this to your vomses file (either the system-wide file or the
> ~/.glite/vomses file):
>
> "fermilab" "fermigrid2.fnal.gov" "15001" "/DC=org/DC=doegrids/
> OU=Services/CN=host/fermigrid2.fnal.gov" "fermilab" "32"
>
>
> NB: fermigrid2.fnal.gov is the same as voms.fnal.gov, the latter
> being an alias to the former.
>
> Then perform your voms-proxy-init thusly:
>
> voms-proxy-init -voms fermilab:/fermilab/minos
>
> As to your question about "What is GUMS?"  GUMS is the grid user
> mapping system and is used at individual sites to map grid users to
> local UIDs.  Think of it as a centralized grid-mapfile service.  It
> just so happens that the Purdue GUMS config file matches the
> Fermilab GUMS config file.   It doesn't necessarily have to be so.
>
> If you have any further questions, please feel free to ask them.
>
> Cheers,
> Dan
