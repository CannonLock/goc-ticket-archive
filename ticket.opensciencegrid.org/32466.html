    OK, I think the latest fixes have succeeded. Let's leave the entries in production. For those interested in the final piece of the puzzle:

The missing hash links for the cilogon-basic files in /etc/grid-security/certificates was indeed a problem. After fixing that, the error did change. If you look at the cilogon-basic.namespaces file in /etc/grid-security/certificates you see

TO Issuer "/DC=org/DC=cilogon/C=US/O=CILogon/CN=CILogon Basic CA 1" \
PERMIT Subject "/DC=org/DC=cilogon/.* "

Note that there is whitespace between the .* and " on the PERMIT line. I looked at the cilogon-osg and cilogon-silver namespaces files, and they don't have that whitespace space. Whether it's a problem or not seems to be depend on what happens down the line for authentication. JINR is using ARGUS, and it seems that the extra whitespace does break things. From Valery:

{{{
2017-01-24 23:26:58.395Z - ERROR [TrustStoreValidationErrorLogger] - Validation error: error at position 4 in chain
, problematic certificate subject: CN=UID:kherner,CN=Kenneth Herner,OU=People,O=Fermi National Accelerator Laborato
ry,C=US,DC=cilogon,DC=org (category: NAMESPACE): The certificate subject CN=UID:kherner,CN=Kenneth Herner,OU=People
,O=Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory,C=US,DC=cilogon,DC=org is not accepted by any rule of the the relevant nam
espace policies. Policies which matches the issuer are: /etc/grid-security/certificates//28776852.namespaces:1
}}}
It is where we see additional space before the end of
PERMIT Subject ...
i.e. it was:
{{{
PERMIT Subject "/DC=org/DC=cilogon/.* "
}}}
apparently it does not affect other methods of certification,
but argus-pepd is more stong here.
So, I removed this extra space now.

After Valery removed the whitespace, I released the jobs held due to the glexec error, and then things started working. I let Dave Dykstra know and I think he was going to pass that along to the maintainers of the CILogon CA (Dave, let me know if you need anything else.)

Ken
