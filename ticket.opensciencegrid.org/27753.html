    Hi Mats,

I found out that there's a VOMS option to prevent it from verifying the issuer, an option "--skipcacheck".  CERN is apparently using this because the CMS VO accepts a Digicert-Grid G2-issued cert even though I am only registered there with my pre-G2 cert.

According to http://italiangrid.github.io/voms/release-notes/voms-admin-server/3.3.2/, VOMS-admin has a similar option called voms.skip_ca_check=True.   This is only for relatively recent versions of VOMS-admin.

An official announcement from IGTF from this past February (at https://www.eugridpma.org/newsletter/eugridpma-newsletter-20150223.txt) requests all VOMS administrators to enable these two options.

    Dec 11, 2015 11:04 PM UTC by Dave Dykstra 
    Kyle,

In case it helps, Digicert-Grid has been issuing SHA-2 certificates to users for a long time.   That's what you see in your own cert.  The recent change was that the CA's own certificate also changed to SHA-2.  That necessitated a DN name change for the issuer.

Also, as Mine said, it takes about a year for all of the old certificates that were issued with the old CA to expire.  As they renew, they'll be issued by the new CA only.
