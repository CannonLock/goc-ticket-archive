    May 13, 2014 03:34 PM UTC 
    Hi  Soichi:

We should probably include Tanya Levshina in this discussion but here is what I know:

There are two methods of replication that are used in Gratia:
1:  collector-to-collector replication, which is controlled in the gratia administration GUI
(in the section that shows Replication summary, Job Usage Replication, and so forth).
We use this method for getting local and regional collectors to send all their data to the central
OSG collector.  Fermilab and Nebraska have such local collectors and I think UCSD still does too.
The collector-based replication works on the premise that the first collector takes the data in,
stores it in the database, and then forwards it to the second collector (with a second independent database)
in an independent replication thread.

There is nothing that would stop you setting up a circular replication in this manner but we have only
ever done this by accident, or in cases where we have set up a temporary copy for a couple weeks.
You would get a huge load of duplicate records in normal operations and
I am not sure the production collector could take it.

1a) I have no idea if you took two different collectors and hooked them up to the
same database, what would happen.  We have never tried that.

2: database to database replication:
Unfortunately with known replication techniques, the gratia schema only allows this to be unidirectional.
We have used multi-master mysql replication here at FermiGrid for years for simpler databases like VOMS
but due to the structure of the database schema (lots of triggers and stored procedures) you can't do that with the gratia database.
We are currently talking to our database administration group here at Fermilab (who recently took over managing the
production OSG gratia database) about newer
mysql-HA techniques that have come out in the past few years but it is at an investigatory stage only.

Hope this answers your questions.
