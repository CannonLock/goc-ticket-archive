    Hi Nate,

We're more than happy to add glow to all possible Fermilab entries. There are a few things I should point out, however:

1. You've compiled a list of Fermilab entries in the factory, but each entry isn't necessarily its own distinct CE. Often times we create entries that point to different queues on the same CE in order to serve special purposes. For instance, "OSG_US_FNAL_ce3" points to the same CE as "CMS_T1_US_FNAL_ce3"; the CMS_T1 entry is for general usage, whereas the OSG entry is a special-purpose entry allowing the OSG VO to send glideins to Fermilab with a different glexec setup. So GLOW is not missing out on anything here.

2. For a lot of these CEs, there's not much we as factory operators can do, because the CEs don't actually support the GLOW VO. I checked BDII for each of the entries you listed, and the following ones don't list GLOW among their supported VOs:
--CMS_T1_US_FNAL_ce2
--CMS_T1_US_FNAL_ce4
--CMS_T1_US_FNAL_ce (same CE as the CMSHTPC entry)
My working understanding of the Fermilab Tier-1 policy is that "ce3" is the designated gatekeeper for all the miscellaneous VOs, while ce, ce2, and ce4 are reserved for CMS glideins only. But if you want to know for sure, you should ask the site admins themselves what their access rules are.

3. These entries (or more precisely, the CEs behind them) currently aren't showing up in BDII, so I can't verify what VOs they support:
--CMS_T3_US_FNALXEN_cms
--Engage_US_FNAL_fnpcosg1
In any case, neither of these entries are really working in the factories; they haven't seen any glideins in over a month.

This leaves only one Fermilab entry that I could add glow to. That entry is "OSGHTPC_US_FNAL_ce3". This entry actually points to the same "ce3" that you already have access to. Adding glow to this entry would just enable you to submit to the HTPC queue, and wouldn't make a difference in terms of normal glideins. If the Fermilab admins are okay with you submitting HTPC glideins here, though, I can definitely enable glowVO for this entry. Just let me know.

When all is said and done, GLOW is basically set up in our factory to submit to all the Fermilab resources that will allow it. If you feel you would like access to more Fermilab resources, again, please contact the site admins. We'll be happy to take care of changing the relevant factory settings once they give you permissions on their side.

Best,
Daniel Klein
UCSD Glidein Factory Operations
