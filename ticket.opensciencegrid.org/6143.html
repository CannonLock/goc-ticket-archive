    Dec 16, 2008 05:28 PM UTC by Arvind Gopu 
    Hi Horst,

I've take an initial stab at fixing all this confused
resource-resource_group mappings.

See here for complete OIM definitions
https://twiki.grid.iu.edu/twiki/bin/view/Operations/OIMTermDefinition ..
I'll try to summarize it here for your convenience:

OIM follows the hierarchy:
* Facility (administrative purposes, not very relevant to admins - for
example, a university)
- Site (administrative purposes, not very relevant to admins - for
example, a lab or a departmentwithin a university)
-- Resource Group (One or more services that run on resources - for
example an admin might choose to group two gatekeepers (CEs) and one SE
as a resource_group
--- Resources - physical machines that run services like CE/SRM/etc.

In your case, (as you pointed out) for some reason, most of your
resources were mapped to the OU_ATLAS site, and consequently
OU_OSCER_ATLAS resource group ... I am not sure who did the mapping.
Perhaps you/Karthik did in the past or perhaps it got into OIM from the
old RegDB migration.

Anyway, what I've done is mapped each resource to be in its own resource
group per one of your suggestions. You're welcome to change it as you
see fit. I also just realized that the current OIM "edit resource"
interface does not allow an admin to change the site, I'll add this to
our to-do list of things to fix. For now, if you need any changes, let
me know.
