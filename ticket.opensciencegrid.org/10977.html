    Oct 4, 2011 06:37 PM UTC by OSG-GOC 
    Scot and I have looked at it. There are a few pieces of evidence:

* When run by itself, the probe took more than 6 minutes of wall clock time

* When looking at the RSV history, sometimes it succeeds and sometimes
it fails.

* Joel says the machines involved are rather busy.

My conclusion is that the timeout from the RSV probe is real. The
machines are heavily loaded and are not always able to respond before
the timeout completes. I think there are two solutions, probably neither
of which are completely satisfying.

Option 1: Make the timeout even bigger.
Option 2: Upgrade such that the machines are better able to handle the
load. (Yes, I realize this is not easy.)

As best we can tell, there isn't a problem with the probe itself.
