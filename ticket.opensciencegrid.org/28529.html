What is (NoCert)?
Skip to end of metadata

Created by Soichi Hayashi, last modified on Apr 08, 2013

Go to start of metadata

If you see above label on your OIM page, it could mean one of following.


    You don't have any X509 Certificate installed on your browser.
You have X509 Certificate installed on your browser, but it's signed by CA that OIM does not trust.
You have X509 Certificate installed on your browser that OIM trusts, but you choose not to provide it.

In case of 1), please submit a new user certificate request. If you already have your certificate (in pkcs12 format), and don't know how to install it on your browser, please see Issuing & Importing.

In case of 2), please make sure that your certificate is signed by IGTF accredited CA. Please visit http://www.igtf.net/ for more information.

In case of 3), please restart your browser and make sure to provide one your X509 certificate while logging into to OIM. Make sure to close all tabs / browsers (not just a page containing OIM) since browser usually keeps its X509 session until restarted and there is no way to force X509 session to be invalidated.

About X509 Certificate

Many website uses user/pass to identify a user (authenticate). One alternative to user/pass authentication is to use X509 Certificate. Since X509 certificates are used to authenticate user to submit jobs on OSG resources, we also use it to authenticate user accessing various OSG web applications including OIM.

For more information on X509 Certificate, please see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X.509
