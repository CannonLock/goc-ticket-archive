Ticket destined for the VDT

1. GOC creates ticket if it doesn't exist. VDT is NOT assigned to the ticket.
2. Instead, GOC sends details of the ticket (Description field) to vdt-support@.... from the GOC
email account. The Subject will include the GOC ticket Title and ticket number.
3. VDT's ticket system, RT, will auto-generate a ticket and send an acknowledgment to the GOC.
4. GOC will edit Footprints ticket
a. Title to include VDT ticket number, [vdt-support #1815], for example.
b. Assign VDT Support Center to the ticket. Option to send email to Assignees is checked.
c. Description updated, ""VDT ticket number added to the Title and VDT added as an Assignee. Please
assign osg@.... to the RT ticket."

Ticket updates will flow in each direction without causing a loop. It should be noted that each time RT
sends an update to the GOC at osg@.... Footprints will email all Assignees and the
Customer of the ticket. Thus, RT will receive a copy of their latest update to the FP ticket.

VDT ticket system, called RT, available at http://vdt.cs.wisc.edu/rt/

    Jun 14, 2006 07:52 PM UTC by Tim Silvers 
    Alain,

As we discussed, the process of ticket creation with the VDT will require a manual process on each
end. If a ticket needs to be assigned to the VDT the GOC will send an email from the GOC account
(NOT Footprints) with the description of the problem, which is included in the existing FP ticket. The
email we send to you will include the FP ticket Title and the ticket number in the form
"ISSUE=####" in the Subject. The email we send will create a ticket in RT and RT will send an
automated acknowledgment to the GOC. The GOC will then edit the ticket in FP to include the RT
ticket number in the Title, assign the VDT Support Center to the ticket as an Assignee, and update
the Description of the ticket to say something like, "VDT ticket number added to the Title and VDT
added as an Assignee." We will choose to email the Assignees and the VDT will receive this update
without sending an ACK back to FP because the RT ticket will exist, and when RT sees the ticket
number in the subject line of the email from FP, it will simply append the email update to the
existing RT ticket. On the RT end, someone will then need make the FP email address
(osg@....) a customer on the ticket so that when you send an update out from the
ticket, it is delivered to FP and appended to the ticket. And yes, FP will send back an ACK to RT,
but RT will simply append this to the description of the RT ticket and not send an ACK back. So
there will be no loop, but the RT ticket will include duplicate information.

We may need a flowchart. :-) So, on this ticket, Alain, you're a customer, so you're going to
receive this email in your personal account. I will copy this update and send it to the VDT Support
Center separately from the GOC account, as described in the process outlined above. Let's do it!!
